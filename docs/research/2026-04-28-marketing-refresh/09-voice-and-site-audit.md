## TL;DR

Homepage scores ~**3.4/5 voice, 2.1/5 wedge, 2.6/5 specificity**. Voice is recognizably Playbook B in spots ("two people, no layers of management," "no questionnaires") but leaks generic SaaS filler in hero, services grid, and CTA reassurance. The biggest failure: the augmentation thesis is nowhere on the page — no line names the third path between Big Consulting and SaaS, no line says "10x more capable," no line names the 33M small-business reality. Rewrite plants the wedge in hero, services title, mission, footer, and kills 10 named clichés.

## Scoring legend

- **Voice (V, 1-5):** Sounds like two named humans in Playbook B register (Basecamp / Sierra / Fathom / Kalzumeus). 1 = SaaS template; 5 = unmistakably Matt or Melissa.
- **Wedge (W, 1-5):** Advances "augmentation, not replacement / third path." 1 = invisible; 5 = explicit.
- **Specificity (S, 1-5):** Concrete vs generic. 1 = appears on any AI site; 5 = only on this one.

Lines ≤3 in any column get a rewrite.

## Top 10 prioritized rewrites

Ranked by leverage (impact × ease).

1. **Hero H1** — "We build tech that *actually helps people.*" → `Make your existing team <em>10x more capable</em> with AI.` — Highest-leverage line on the site; plants the wedge above the fold.
2. **Hero subhead** — cost-only framing → third-path framing (see §2). "Keep the people who built this" carries augmentation for non-technical buyers.
3. **Mission ¶3 insertion** — Add the 33M / third-path paragraph. Most compounding addition; reframes every other claim.
4. **CTA reassurance** — "No obligation…" → handoff U16 verbatim. Highest-trust single line you can ship.
5. **Hero eyebrow** — "Technology with a purpose" → `For the 33M small businesses without an AI team.` Filler → concrete TAM that is also the wedge.
6. **Services section title** — "AI that works for your business…" → `AI that <em>amplifies your team</em> — not replaces it.`
7. **Hero visual** — Delete fake JS code; replace per Batch G U33. Loudest off-brand element on the page.
8. **Service Card 3 title** — "AI Agents That Run Your Operations" → `AI agents that work alongside your team` — removes the only grid line that contradicts the wedge.
9. **Process Step 3 title** — "Your World Gets Better" → `Your team takes the wheel` — direct augmentation outcome.
10. **Team bios** — "25 years…" / "Two decades…" → named companies, named venues, concrete operator tests. Blocked on handoff §7 facts; ship structure now.

## Surfaces beyond the homepage that also need work

By leverage:

1. **OG image** (`og.png`, `og-square.png`) — Currently CTA pill only. Re-render with wedge headline: `Make your team 10x more capable with AI.`
2. **OG / Twitter description** — Lead with augmentation thesis once hero subhead lands.

4. **JSON-LD descriptions** (Organization + ProfessionalService) — Append: `…to augment small-business teams without adding headcount or replacing the people doing the work.`
5. **FAQ (U13)** — Two of the six questions must carry the wedge: "Will this replace people on my team?" and "Why hire you instead of Big-4 / a SaaS tool?"
6. **Disqualification block (U15)** — "Not a fit" column is the highest-leverage wedge-by-negative-space: enterprises, anyone hoping to replace headcount, anyone who wants a chatbot for chatbot's sake.
7. **Pricing block (U18)** — Framing: "We charge for outcomes — your team, ten times more capable. Not for seats, not for tokens, not for managed services."
8. **Sample Builds (U20)** — Title each Loom in augmentation language: "How [client]'s 3-person ops team handles 5x volume" not "AI automation case study."
9. **Social bios** — LinkedIn / future IG / future X. Template: `AI consulting for small business. We make your existing team 10x more capable. Two people, no layers of management. SoFL.`
10. **README.md + handoff §1 TL;DR** — Both currently describe GVC as "AI consulting for small businesses." Append "— augmentation, not replacement" so every future agent inherits the thesis at the top of the repo.
