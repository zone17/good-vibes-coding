## 1. Elite Agency Design Benchmarks

Drawing from top AI-consulting, boutique dev agency, and technical consultancy landing pages in 2026.

### Elite Table-Stakes (top 25%)

- **Custom or distinctive typography.** Linear (Inter Display tuned), Vercel (Geist), Stripe (Sohne), Anthropic (Styrene + Tiempos). System fonts and Roboto read as default.
- **One dominant hero visual**, not a grid of features. Full-bleed or single composition. Text column narrow, anchored to a calm area.
- **Committed palette:** one dominant surface + one sharp accent. No evenly-distributed rainbows.
- **Tight spacing scale** (4 or 8 px base), consistent radii (6–12 px), consistent stroke weights on icons.
- **Purposeful motion:** one entrance sequence, one scroll-linked effect, one hover state. Smooth at 60 fps, mobile-safe.
- **Semantic HTML + visible focus rings.** Accessibility is elite-signal, not overhead.
- **Real imagery or generated-with-intent imagery** — not stock, not gradient blobs.

### Elite Differentiators (top 5%)

- **A single memorable "thing"** — the Linear glow, Stripe's live shader gradient, Vercel's compute-grid hero, Anthropic's serif/sans tension, Clerk's dimensional component previews, Raycast's product-in-motion hero. One visual you can describe in 5 words after closing the tab.
- **Product-as-hero**, not illustration-as-hero. Mux, Modal, Railway, Sentry render actual UI/logs/dashboards to pixel-perfection. Code blocks, when used (Modal, Fly, Pinecone), are *runnable looking* with syntax tokens, cursor, subtle chrome — not screenshots of `<pre>`.
- **Micro-detail density:** cursor trails (Vercel), hover-magnetism on CTAs (Linear), staggered reveal on scroll (Stripe sessions), scroll-linked number counters (Mux). Individually invisible, collectively elite.
- **Editorial typography moves:** oversized display type that breaks out of grid (Anthropic, Sierra), serif/sans mixing with intent, true optical sizing, tabular numerals in pricing.
- **Dark mode that isn't black:** Linear's near-black blue, Raycast's warm charcoal, Vercel's true-black-with-noise. Pure `#000` is a tell.
- **Deliberate color grading on images:** consistent LUT across team photos, product shots, illustrations. Fathom and Sierra do this well.

### Dated Patterns to Avoid (2026 cliches)

- Gradient blobs / mesh gradients behind hero (2021 aesthetic)
- "Powerful. Simple. Fast." triptych headers
- Logo cloud above the fold ("Trusted by…") before the value prop lands
- Stock photos of laptops, handshakes, diverse-team-at-whiteboard
- Glassmorphism cards stacked on a gradient
- Card grid of 3–6 services as the hero impression
- Dark mode by default without reason
- Inter / Space Grotesk / Roboto as display font (reads as template)
- Decorative code blocks that contain fake/nonsense code
- Purple-on-white, the universal 2020–2023 SaaS palette
- Centered headline + two pill CTAs + screenshot below (Framer-template look)

### 6 Sites to Study

- **Linear (linear.app)** — Restraint as craft. One hue, one accent, world-class type rhythm, product screenshots as hero.
- **Vercel (vercel.com)** — Live compute visual in hero; every interaction has micro-motion; true black + neon accent done right.
- **Anthropic (anthropic.com)** — Editorial serif/sans pairing, warm palette, research-lab gravitas without stock imagery.
- **Modal (modal.com)** — Runnable-feeling code block as hero visual; dense but breathable; monospace accents.
- **Sierra (sierra.ai)** — Boutique AI-consultancy benchmark: oversized editorial type, muted palette, restrained motion, team credibility without stock photos.
- **Raycast (raycast.com)** — Product-in-motion hero, immaculate dark mode, obsessive micro-detail on hover/focus states.
- **Clerk (clerk.com)** — Dimensional component previews (floating, lit) — elite way to show product without dashboard screenshots.
- **Fathom (usefathom.com)** — Proof that a small team can feel elite: one confident typeface, genuine illustrations, no SaaS cliches.

## 2. Conversion Optimization for B2B Service Landing Pages

For consulting/agency sites where the primary conversion is a booked discovery call (Calendly).

### Top 5 Conversion Levers (in priority order)

1. **Hero clarity in <5 seconds.** Specific, benefit-driven H1 that names *who it's for* and *what changes for them*. Generic "AI consulting" loses; "AI automation for small businesses tired of repetitive work" wins. Clarity outranks every other lever — a confused visitor never books.
2. **Primary CTA above the fold, singular.** One CTA visible without scrolling can lift conversion dramatically (cited: +317%). Every secondary CTA above the fold dilutes it. Repeat the *same* CTA 3–5x down the page.
3. **Proof density near the CTA.** Social proof adjacent to action points converts better than a dedicated "proof" section. For a no-logos shop: stack founder credibility, specific demoed outcomes, unedited X/LinkedIn screenshots beside every CTA.
4. **Friction reduction on the booking step.** Embed Calendly inline (no redirect). Ask only name + email + one qualifying question. Every added field can cost ~17% of conversions; 3-fields-or-fewer is the 2026 norm.
5. **Objection handling before the ask.** A short FAQ or "Is this for you?" block that names the exact fears (cost, AI hype fatigue, "burned before") converts skeptical buyers who are otherwise one doubt away from closing the tab.

### Common Conversion Leaks

- Vague value prop ("we help you leverage AI") — no specificity, no audience, no outcome.
- Multiple competing CTAs (newsletter + demo + contact + download) — splits attention, kills the primary metric.
- Proof section all stock photos or generic "trusted by" without names.
- Pricing black box — 2026 buyers expect at least a range. Hiding it signals "we'll quote based on how rich you look."
- No process transparency — buyers burned by agencies want to see *exactly* what happens after they book.
- Founder anonymity — faceless brand pages underperform founder-fronted pages by 15–28% in 2025–2026 data.
- Slow mobile LCP and redirect-based Calendly flow.

### Modern Patterns That Predict Booked Calls

- **Founder-hosted 60–90s video** near the hero (iPhone quality > polished agency productions).
- **Loom walkthrough** of an actual build/demo as stand-in for case studies.
- **Stacked micro-testimonials** from X, LinkedIn, email screenshots — unedited and verifiable.
- **Before/after metric cards** with specific numbers ("12 hrs/week → 2 hrs/week on invoicing").
- **Transparent process block** — "Day 1 we do X, Week 1 we ship Y" — reduces the unknown.
- **Anti-sales language** — "No pressure. No pitch deck. If we're not a fit, we'll tell you in the first 10 minutes."
- **Pricing transparency** — even "typical engagements $X–$Y" outperforms "contact for pricing."

### Specific Recommendations for a 2-Person Consultancy with No Client List

1. **Lead with a narrow, named audience.** "AI automation for small business owners and solo operators" — not "enterprises." Claim the niche your size is a feature in.
2. **Replace missing logos with a founder proof stack:** two real founder photos + names + 60-second iPhone video explaining *why you started this and who you refuse to work with.*
3. **Build a "Sample Builds" section** (not case studies) — 2–3 Loom videos of AI workflows you've built (for yourselves, a friend, pro-bono). Before/after screenshots.
4. **Add an explicit "Is this for you / not for you" block.** "Good fit: $200K–$5M revenue, losing 5+ hrs/week to repetitive work. Not a fit: enterprises, anyone wanting a chatbot for the sake of it."
5. **Anti-sales CTA copy.** "Book a free 30-min call — no pitch, no pressure. If AI isn't right for you, we'll say so."
6. **Publish a price floor.** Even "Pilots from $2–5K" filters tire-kickers and earns trust that "contact us" destroys.
7. **Embed Calendly inline** below the hero and repeat it 2–3x down the page. No redirect, no modal gate.
8. **Add an FAQ block** answering: "I've been burned before, how are you different?" / "What if AI isn't right for us?" / "What does the first 30 days look like?" / "What does it cost?"
9. **One page, one CTA.** Kill nav links that compete with booking. Keep phone number as secondary trust signal, not a competing action.
10. **Show the two humans.** Names, faces, LinkedIn links, one-line "why we do this" each.

## 3. SEO / Technical Marketing Site Fundamentals (2026)

For a just-launched static HTML+CSS site targeting "AI consulting for small business," "AI automation consultant," "custom software for small business."

### P0 — Ship this week

1. **Open Graph + Twitter Card meta tags** — every LinkedIn/iMessage/Slack share is currently blank. Required: `og:title`, `og:description`, `og:image`, `og:url`, `og:type`, `og:site_name`, `twitter:card: summary_large_image`, canonical link.
2. **Static OG image at `/og.png`** — 1200×630, <1MB, brand mark + tagline + URL, readable at 300×157 thumbnail. Static beats dynamic (`@vercel/og`) for a one-page site. Duplicate `og-square.png` (1200×1200) for iMessage/WhatsApp.
3. **JSON-LD structured data** (Google rich results + 2026 AI Overviews). Add three blocks:
   - `Organization` with `name`, `url`, `logo`, `sameAs` (LinkedIn), `contactPoint` (phone + email)
   - `LocalBusiness` (Palm Beach / South Florida — `ProfessionalService`, `areaServed`, `telephone`)
   - `FAQPage` — pull 4–6 objections from content
4. **`robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml`** — required for Google/Bing/ChatGPT/Perplexity crawlers. `robots.txt` should explicitly allow `GPTBot`, `ClaudeBot`, `PerplexityBot`, `Google-Extended`.
5. **Favicon completeness.** SVG-only breaks Safari pinned tabs, iOS home-screen, older browsers. Already shipped (2026-04-17): `favicon.ico` (32×32 multi-res), `favicon.svg`, `apple-touch-icon.png` (180×180, no transparency), `icon-192.png` + `icon-512.png`, `site.webmanifest`, `theme-color`.
6. **Analytics.** Recommend **Plausible** ($9/mo) or **Fathom** ($15/mo) over GA4. No cookie banner, 1KB script, dead-simple dashboard. Skip GA4 unless running Google Ads. Add **Vercel Web Analytics** (free) for Core Web Vitals field data.

### P1 — Within 2 weeks

7. **Core Web Vitals fixes.** 2026 targets: LCP <2.5s, INP <200ms, CLS <0.1. Likely misses:
   - Google Fonts is the LCP villain. Self-host WOFF2 (drop preconnects, save 200–400ms), or `<link rel="preload" as="font" type="font/woff2" crossorigin>` for the H1 weight.
   - `team.jpg` — serve as AVIF/WebP with `<picture>` fallback.
   - Hero CSS animations — audit for `will-change` overuse causing INP regressions.
8. **Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster Tools** — submit sitemap, verify via DNS TXT. Free. Query impressions within ~3 days.

### P2 — Within 1 month (content SEO)

9. **Break single-page for SEO.** Single-page can't rank for multiple intents. Minimum:
   - `/services/ai-automation-for-small-business/`
   - `/services/custom-software-for-small-business/`
   - `/services/ai-agents/`
   - `/about/` (expanded team bios → `Person` schema + E-E-A-T)
   Each 800–1500 words, one H1, semantic headings, internal links to CTA.
10. **Location page** — `/palm-beach-ai-consulting/` or `/locations/south-florida/`. LocalBusiness schema + GBP listing drives "near me" + map-pack traffic.
11. **Blog: narrowly.** Skip the content treadmill. 6–10 evergreen pillar posts answering actual sales-call questions: "What does AI automation cost for a small business?", "AI agents vs. Zapier vs. custom software", "How to pick an AI consultant." Doubles as sales enablement.

### Skip / Deprioritize

- `Service` and `Person` JSON-LD (low return until dedicated pages exist)
- Schema generators / WordPress-style SEO plugins (overkill for static HTML)
- GA4 (unless running Google Ads)
- AMP (dead)

## 4. Trust Signals for Brand-New Consultancies

The hardest problem for a just-launched services business: building trust *before* client logos, case studies, or testimonials exist.

### Day-One Trust Assets

1. **Founder provenance on the hero/about section.** "Formerly at [prior company]" with verifiable LinkedIn links outperforms generic "20+ years experience." Matt: name the engineering orgs he led. Melissa: name the hospitality brands she ran ops for. Specificity = credibility.
2. **Pricing transparency.** Published starting prices or engagement tiers ("Discovery Sprint: $X fixed fee, 2 weeks") signals confidence. Vague "contact us" reads as desperate or extractive. Basecamp, Thoughtbot, Kalzumeus publish rates.
3. **Process transparency.** A "How we work" page with concrete phases, deliverables, decision points. Substitutes for case studies — showing the *method* instead of the *outcome*.
4. **Risk reversal.** Money-back guarantee on discovery sprint, or "cancel anytime" monthly engagement. Patrick McKenzie (Kalzumeus) signature move.
5. **WikiFold as competence proof.** Already live. Put it front-and-center: "Here's something we built — use it free." Single strongest signal currently available.
6. **Founder photos, real names, real phone number.** Already present. Real photos beat illustrations for trust (Baymard Institute research).

### Founder Credentialing Patterns

- **LinkedIn deep-link from every founder mention.** Lets prospects independently verify tenure/titles — highest-leverage trust move.
- **Personal bio depth.** 150–250 words covering what they did, what they shipped, what they learned, why this firm exists. Generic "passionate about AI" destroys trust.
- **90-second Loom video intro** on homepage. Faces + voices compress weeks of trust-building. Derek Sivers builds his entire brand on this. Cost: one afternoon.
- **Cross-disciplinary positioning as a feature.** "Engineer + Operator" is unusually legible. Lean into it — most AI shops are two engineers who've never run a P&L.

### 3-Month Proof-Stack Roadmap

**Month 1 — Publish in public.**
- Launch a newsletter or Substack. Two posts: (1) What we learned building WikiFold, (2) AI integration mistakes we see hospitality/enterprise teams making. Nat Eliason and Paul Jarvis built entire businesses newsletter-first.
- Create a GitHub org. Open-source one small utility from WikiFold's internals.

**Month 2 — Stake expertise publicly.**
- Two more essays with opinionated POV (Kalzumeus-style: specific, technical, generous). Post to Hacker News, r/LocalLLaMA, LinkedIn.
- Guest on 1–2 podcasts in hospitality-tech or AI-for-SMB niches. Easier to book when you have a specific POV.

**Month 3 — Productize the first engagement.**
- Offer a fixed-scope, fixed-price "AI Readiness Audit" ($5–15k, 2 weeks). Even one paid engagement = one case study (anonymized if needed).
- Add a "Writing" section linking to essays, podcasts, GitHub.

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Fake social proof.** No stock-photo testimonials, "Trusted by 1000+ teams," AI-generated client logos. Prospects Google everything.
- **Vague credential inflation.** "25+ years combined experience" without named companies reads as hiding.
- **Corporate-we voice.** Day-one agencies should write first-person plural or name the founder. "Our team of experts" when there are two of you is detected immediately.
- **Generic AI logos/stock imagery.** Purple gradients and abstract neural nets signal "no differentiation."
- **Badge theater.** Don't add "AWS Partner" / "Certified" badges unless actually current. Easily verified, catastrophic when fake.
- **Hiding the small team size.** "Two-person boutique" is a *feature* for buyers burned by big-agency bait-and-switch. Own it.
