## TL;DR — recommended top 3 + backups

The three sprints in `17` ($4.5K / $7.5K / $6.5K) were priced for warm referrals. **For cold acquisition into cash-light SMBs paying $40/mo for Squarespace, those won't clear the first conversation.** Cold-conversion floor for a first sprint with a stranger is $300–$1,500; ceiling before proof exists is ~$3,000.

**Ship these three in the next 30 days:**

1. **AI Visibility Tune-Up — $750 flat (5 days)** — GBP claim/optimize, JSON-LD schema, stub FAQ, AI-search audit ("does ChatGPT know you exist?"). Cold-bait. Ladders to $250/mo Visibility Watch.
2. **Menu+Reviews Sprint (lite) — $1,500 flat (1 week)** — one-page custom-URL menu/amenity sheet, weekly AI review-response drafts, FL-allergen or Poconos pet/accessibility tagging. Ladders to $300/mo Hospitality Ops Lite.
3. **AI Front-Desk Sprint — $1,800 setup + $200/mo (2 weeks)** — custom Twilio+LLM receptionist + SMS auto-responder, priced *under* Goodcall/Numa/Rosie ($49–$79/mo SaaS). Built-in retainer from day 1.

**Backups (months 2–4 if top 3 underconvert):** Vacation Rental Direct-Booking Site ($2,400, Pocs-led, Hostfully-killer); SOP Lite ($2,200, $300/mo wiki keep-alive).

**Refused:** AI quoting (ServiceTitan moat too deep), AI social (off-brand), email migration (off-vertical for both markets), KB for CS (collapses into SOP Lite), AI inventory (POS-locked), AI booking replacement (vertical SaaS wins; refer out).

**Net effect on $20K MRR:** credible by month **10–11** on this portfolio — only because *every* top-3 product has a recurring tail by design. The sprint isn't the revenue; the tail is. Sprint price clears the first cold conversation; retainer ARPU compounds.

## Constraints driving the portfolio choices

Six brief constraints collapse to three load-bearing rules:

1. **Price clears cold conversion, not max margin/sprint.** $750–$2,500 is where strangers say yes on call #1. Original $4.5K–$7.5K is right for warm, wrong for cold. Headline price shifts ~60% down.
2. **Built-in retainer, not "we'll talk later."** Cash-light SMBs won't commit to $1.5K/mo from a $4.5K sprint. They *will* commit to $200–$300/mo if it visibly replaces a *named* SaaS line item they already pay for. Anti-SaaS framing is doing real work — every starter must name what it replaces.
3. **Mizner Park AND Main St Narrowsburg.** Kills geography-specific products as headlines (SB-68 allergen play works FL/CA, dies PA/NY — keep as segment add-on). Privileges shared SMB behaviors: phone calls, online discovery, reviews, bookings.

Carry from `17`: 2-person delivery, 1–3 week productized cycle, template-shippable; no fire-your-staff framing; no Bronze/Silver/Gold.

## Detailed product specs (top 3)

### Product 1: AI Visibility Tune-Up — $750 flat (5 days)

**Cold pitch:** "Most SMBs are invisible to ChatGPT, Google AI Overview, and Perplexity. We fix that in 5 business days for $750 — flat fee, you keep everything."

**Included:** GBP claim + optimize (categories, hours, services, photos, Q&A seeded); JSON-LD schema added to existing site (LocalBusiness/FAQPage/Restaurant/LodgingBusiness — works on Squarespace/Wix/WordPress without rebuild); one stub FAQ page with 10 owner-voice answers from a 30-min interview; AI-search audit (5 prompts run pre/post against ChatGPT/Perplexity/Google AI Overview, side-by-side report); source files + 1-page maintenance checklist.

**Cold-conversion mechanics:** $750 sits squarely in the cold-comfort band. Prospect demo: run the AI-search audit on *their* business *before* the call — built-in proof, no deck. 5-day cycle + Stripe link at signature = paid invoice within 7 days of first contact.

**First 5 customers (named):**
1. Boca Raton indie restaurant on East Palmetto Park Rd (single-location, owner-op — Mel's network)
2. West Palm Beach Clematis St café or boutique
3. Narrowsburg Main St gallery/café (14764 zip — visitor economy)
4. Honesdale PA (Wayne County) inn-keeper or shop
5. Hawley/Lake Wallenpaupack visitor-services SMB (charter, rental, restaurant)

**1-week MVP:** homepage adds the offer card; one 90s Loom of Matt running the audit on a real anonymized business (before/after delta); Stripe payment link; templated deliverable = ~6 hrs work/engagement at template-leverage.

**Ladder:** offer **Visibility Watch — $250/mo** (monthly AI-search recheck, GBP post+photo, schema updates, quarterly review of new AI-discovery surfaces). Conversion target **30%**. Or upsell to Product 2/3 at sprint completion (~25%).

### Product 2: Menu+Reviews Sprint (Lite) — $1,500 flat (1 week)

**Cold pitch:** "We rebuild your menu (or amenity sheet) as one source of truth, draft your review responses in your voice, and tag your allergens (FL) or pet/accessibility info (Pocs) — $1,500, one week, you keep the code."

**Included:** one-page custom-URL menu (restaurant) or amenity sheet (B&B/inn) on a fast static site, owner-editable; allergen tagging w/ cross-contamination disclaimers (FL, pre-empts SB-68 spread) or pet/accessibility/quiet-hours tagging (Poconos); AI review-response system (weekly email digest with 3–5 owner-voice drafts, one-click approve, auto-post); GBP cleanup (carry-over from #1); brand-voice rules doc (Mel writes the venue rules).

**Cold-conversion mechanics:** $1,500 is the upper edge of cold-comfort. Pitch lands as named-SaaS replacement: "You pay $30 to OpenMenu, $99 to Bloom, $300 to Birdeye — three menus still drift, reviews go unanswered. We replace all three with code you own, $1,500 once + $300/mo we run." 67% cheaper than the warm-motion price in `17` because scope is narrower (single page, weekly batching, no full-stack rebuild — that's the upsell).

**First 5 customers (named):**
1. Delray Atlantic Ave indie restaurant (allergen-forward base)
2. Fort Lauderdale Las Olas single-location restaurant
3. Lake Worth/Lantana family-owned spot ("owner does six menus by hand")
4. Narrowsburg/Callicoon NY Main St restaurant (Sullivan County)
5. Hawley/Honesdale PA inn or B&B (Wayne County) — amenity-sheet variant

**1-week MVP:** homepage card; "Bistro Mizner" sample menu (specced in `17`) public+linkable; "Lake Wallenpaupack Inn" amenity sample with pet/quiet-hours/accessibility public+linkable; 90s Mel-led Loom on both variants; Calendly disqualifies chains >3 locations.

**Ladder:** **Hospitality Ops Lite — $300/mo** (monthly menu/amenity refresh, weekly review-batch, quarterly allergen/policy audit, named human). Conversion target **45%** — engineered for it; review-response quality decays without monthly tuning. Upsell to full $4,500 Sprint or $1,500/mo Hospitality Retainer at month 3 if venue grows.

### Product 3: AI Front-Desk Sprint — $1,800 setup + $200/mo (2 weeks)

**Cold pitch:** "We replace the $59/mo AI receptionist SaaS you'd buy with a custom one we build in 2 weeks — $1,800 to build, $200/mo to run, you own the prompts and the call data."

**Included:** Twilio number (port or new); LLM-powered phone agent that answers, qualifies, books to Cal.com or owner SMS, escalates via warm transfer or "we'll call you back in 5"; SMS auto-responder for after-hours and missed calls; owner's actual FAQs + service list + booking rules as prompt corpus (the moat vs Goodcall — *their* voice and rules, not a template); read-only call log + transcript dashboard. Monthly retainer covers hosting, LLM API, Twilio, 1 prompt-tuning session, escalation review.

**Cold-conversion mechanics:** strongest anti-SaaS pitch in the portfolio — prospect already knows Goodcall/Numa/Rosie exist ($49–$79/mo), offer is "we build what they sell, you own it, same recurring spend." Setup fee covers ~16 hrs at template-leverage (Twilio + LLM + Cal.com scaffold reusable across ~80% of customers). $200/mo is sized at the SaaS price the prospect was already mentally budgeting — *fastest path to $20K MRR in the portfolio because every sale is recurring from day 1*. Vertical fit is broad: HVAC missing 30% of after-hours calls, dental losing new-patient calls at lunch, salon owner doing color, B&B owner asleep at 2am.

**First 5 customers (named):**
1. Boca Raton/Boynton Beach HVAC contractor (3–10 trucks, owner-takes-calls pain)
2. West Palm Beach single-location dental practice (lunch-hour + post-5pm gaps)
3. Delray salon/spa (owner doing color, can't answer)
4. Honesdale PA contractor/HVAC (Wayne County — small ops, no front-desk staff)
5. Narrowsburg/Lake Wallenpaupack B&B owner (off-site or asleep when calls come in)

**1-week MVP:** homepage card; live demo number cold prospects can call (agent for "Bistro Mizner" or "Pocono Plumbing", real SMS confirmation back); 2-min Matt Loom of the full call flow → SMS → dashboard; Twilio+Cal.com+LLM starter repo (2-day setup/customer at template-leverage).

**Ladder:** *already a retainer.* $200/mo from day 1, monthly Stripe. Month-2 upsell: "what about replying to texts the same way?" → +$100/mo SMS tier. Month-6 upsell into the $2,500/mo Operations Retainer once ROI numbers exist.

## $20K MRR math given the recommended portfolio

Assumptions (defensible against `18` cold-conv benchmarks and `04`/`17` retainer data):

- **Cold outreach top-of-funnel:** 2 founders × 25 touches/wk = ~200/mo (email + LinkedIn DM + warm-intro-via-Chamber/SBDC)
- **Cold-touch → 30-min call:** 6% (industry baseline 2–4%; we're priced low and the offer is concrete)
- **Call → sprint sale:** 25% weighted across top 3 (#1 ~35%, #3 ~15% — $2K cold commit harder than $750)
- **Sprint → retainer take-rate:** weighted 35% (#3 is 100% built-in; #2 is 45%; #1 is 30%)
- **Retainer churn:** 10%/mo first 90 days, settling to 4–6%/mo by month 6
- **Avg retainer ARPU:** $260/mo (weighted: #1 $250, #2 $300, #3 $200, Backup A $250)

**Pure-cold ramp (no warm assist, no bundles):**

| Month | Touches | Calls | Sprints | New retainers | Net retainers (after churn) | MRR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (May 2026) | 200 | 12 | 3 | 1 | 1 | $260 |
| 3 (Jul) | 250 | 15 | 4 | 2 | 4 | $1,040 |
| 6 (Oct) | 300 | 18 | 5 | 2 | 10 | $2,600 |
| 10 (Feb) | 400 | 24 | 7 | 3 | 22 | $5,720 |

**Pure-cold hits ~$5.7K MRR by month 10 — credible but nowhere near $20K.** Hitting $20K requires two layers on top:

1. **Warm motion reactivates at month 3+** (podcast + 3 case studies exist). Warm sprint→retainer converts ~2× cold (per `09`/`18`).
2. **Bundle pricing fronts at month 3** ($500/mo Restaurant Full Stack, $300/mo B&B Full Stack). Bundle ARPU ~$400/mo = 1.5× standalone.

**Combined warm+cold+bundle ramp:**

| Month | Sprints | Bundle % | New MRR | Cumulative |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3 | 0% | $260 | $260 |
| 3 | 4 | 20% | $400 | $920 |
| 5 | 7 | 40% | $1,260 | $2,880 |
| 6 | 9 | 50% | $1,800 | $4,680 |
| 8 | 14 | 60% | $3,150 | $10,230 |
| 10 | 20 | 60% | $4,500 | **$18,555** |
| 11 | 22 | 60% | $4,950 | **$23,505** |

**Realistic $20K MRR: month 11 (March–April 2027).** Roughly tied with `18`'s warm-only "$15K+ MRR by month 9–10," but distribution is more honest: cold *carries volume*; warm *converts bundle rate*; podcast *makes cold touches warm enough to convert at 6% not 2%*.

**Fastest single lever: Product 3.** 50 Front-Desk customers alone = $10K MRR + $90K of paid-up-front sprint revenue. If cold converts #3 at the 4–5% AI-receptionist-SaaS cold-ad benchmark, **$20K MRR by month 7–8** is the upside case worth swinging for.

**Downside case:** founders haven't done outbound at scale. If by Day 45 (mid-June) sprint sales are <2/wk, switch to warm motion (`17`/`18`) for sprints; keep cold only for #1 ($750) as top-of-funnel filler.
