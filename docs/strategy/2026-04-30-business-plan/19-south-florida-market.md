## TL;DR

Palm Beach County (PBC): 1.56M residents (2026), median HHI $84,921, metro SMBs = **53.6% of employment**. Bottom-up estimate **~45,000–50,000 employer firms** across the nine target cities. Six cold-target cohorts in priority: (1) **multi-unit hospitality / F&B** (~5,500 food-svc licenses, Mel moat), (2) **home services** with snowbird urgency, (3) **boutique professional services** (~3,000+ law / CPA / RIA / dental), (4) **med spas / wellness** (high marketing spend, weak ops), (5) **marine** (211 firms, $386.6M GRP, March boat-show urgency), (6) **real estate** (93K agents post-MIAMI/RWorld merger — too crowded to cold-pitch, use as referral). Local agencies charge $2–15K/site and $1.5–7K/mo retainers — **none** position as "AI-native, custom-tooling-replaces-SaaS." That's GVC's wedge. Highest-leverage cold channels: Greater Boca Raton Chamber (~1,500 members, 300+ events/yr), FRLA Palm Beach Chapter, BNI Boca/Delray/WPB, MIAPBC, 1909 coworking, and 6 anchor events (PBIBS, Festival of the Arts BOCA, Delray Affair, Savor the Avenue, ArtiGras, Palm Beach Food & Wine).

**GVC's wedge against all three tiers:**
1. **"We replace SaaS, we don't add SaaS."** Local agencies sell *more* tools (HubSpot, Klaviyo, Mailchimp); GVC sells *fewer subscriptions* via custom AI tools. Anti-SaaS framing is unique here.
2. **Engineering depth.** Matt's 25 yrs vs. agencies that subcontract dev = credibility moat with operators burned by cheap dev.
3. **Operator vocabulary.** Mel can sit in front of a Toast P&L and identify the actual margin leak. Agencies sell channels; GVC sells operations.

## Local marketing channels — costs and what works

**Print** — best for *credibility halo*, weak for direct lead-gen at GVC's price point:
- **Palm Beach Daily News** ("Shiny Sheet") — Town of PB society/luxury readership; ultra-luxury only
- **Palm Beach Post** — display $25–60 CPM digital, $3–15K full-page print; SMB directory $200–500/mo ([Palm Beach Post Advertise](https://go.palmbeachpost.localiq.com/))
- **Boca Magazine** — 45+ yr CVC-audited monthly; full page ~$4–8K, 1/4 page $1.5–3K ([bocamag.com/advertise](https://bocamag.com/advertise/))
- **Boca Raton Observer** — 152K print + digital ([2025 Media Kit](https://bocaratonobserver.com/downloads/24844/download/2025%20Boca%20Raton%20Observer%20Magazine%20Media%20Kit.pdf))
- **Palm Beach Illustrated** — luxury monthly, ~$5–10K/page ([2025 Media Kit](https://palmbeachmedia.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/PBI_2025_MediaKit.pdf))
- **Atlantic Ave Magazine, Delray Newspaper** — neighborhood scale, sub-$1K units common

**Radio:** WJNO (AM news/talk), Sunny 107.9, X102.3, Hot 105.5. 30-sec spots $50–250 daypart. Useful for home services; weak for advisory.

**Local TV:** WPTV (NBC), WPBF (ABC), WPEC CBS12. 30-sec $250–2K+. Not a fit for GVC's price/buyer.

**Geo-targeted Google / Meta:** National avg Google Ads CPC $5.26 (2025); legal/finance $8+ ([Focus Digital 2025 CPC report](https://focus-digital.co/average-google-ads-cost-per-click-by-industry/)). PBC LSAs $28/lead (low end), Search Ads $47/lead in same case study; "Boca Raton attorney" CPC $25–45; "WPB HVAC" $15–25; "pool cleaning Boca" $8–14 ([Atomic Social PBC ROI](https://atomicsocial.com/google-ads-roi-strategies-for-palm-beach-county-fl-businesses/), [BullsEye on LSAs](https://bullseyeinternet.com/show-me-the-money-decoding-google-local-service-ads-pricing/)). Meta CPC for geo-targeted "small business [service]" $1.50–3.50.

**Verdict for GVC:** Best paid channel = **LinkedIn ads to "Owner / Founder, 5–50 employees, Palm Beach County"** + Meta retargeting. $1.5–3K/mo cap. Direct paid acquisition for *advisory services* almost never beats earned/owned for a 2-person shop — confirm via case studies + Boca Mag-grade earned media.

**Direct mail** — snowbird homeowners (Oct–Apr) respond well: $0.45–0.85/piece, 0.5–2% response for home services. Useless for B2B advisory.

**Local podcast advertising:** No PBC podcast has scale today. Opportunity is to *be on them*, not advertise on them.

## Buying triggers and seasonality

**Snowbird cycle (the dominant driver):**
- Pre-season ramp Aug–Oct: hospitality, home services, retail prepping — strong window for "ready by November" cold pitches.
- Peak Nov–Apr: cash flowing, owners busy. Use for *referral conversion*, not cold acquisition.
- Post-season May–Jul: owners doing post-mortems, planning next-season investments. **Single best cold-outreach window.**

Sources: [BEBR Florida Snowbird study](https://bebr.ufl.edu/sites/default/files/Research%20Reports/snowbirds_0.pdf), [Florida Realty Marketplace](https://www.floridarealtymarketplace.com/blog/all-about-snowbird-season-in-florida.html).

**Hurricane prep (Jun–Nov):** short urgency for property services, insurance-tech, tenant-comms automation.

**Regulatory:** **Florida Digital Bill of Rights** (eff. Jul 1, 2024) directly binds only $1B+ firms but cascades to SMBs handling Floridian sensitive personal data via vendor-compliance contract clauses ([White & Case](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-alert/florida-enacts-digital-bill-rights-joining-growing-privacy-landscape), [Patel Law SMB summary](https://www.flpatellaw.com/florida-digital-bill-of-rights-sb-262/)). Modest pitch hook for med spas + prof services. FL restaurant allergen-disclosure updates open guest-CRM / menu-tech pitch.

**Industry cycles:** Charter operators ramp Jan–Mar for PBIBS. Wellington equestrian (WEF/AGDF) peaks Dec–Apr; quiet May–Sep. CPA tax crunch Dec–Apr — **May is the only good cold-call window**. Medical credentialing peaks Jan + Jul. **Real estate post-merger (May 2026): brokerages reorganizing tooling for 6–12 mo — unique 12-month window.**

## GVC's strongest cold-pitch angles for this market

1. **"Cut your SaaS bill in half by next snowbird season."** Target: $1–5M home services, hospitality, multi-unit retail. Audit current stack (Toast + Resy + Mailchimp + Klaviyo + Boulevard + Mindbody…), replace 3–5 with one custom tool, deliver before November.
2. **"Replace your front desk with an AI agent — without losing your hospitality."** Target: boutique hotels, B&Bs, med spas, dental, charter ops. Mel's vocabulary owns this.
3. **"Build the post-merger ops layer for the new RWorld/MIAMI brokerage world."** Brokerages reorganizing for 6–12 mo need CRM/lead-routing/comp stitching. Cold-pitch broker-owners via PBBOR.
4. **"Stop paying an agency for ad reports nobody reads. We build the dashboard."** Target operators currently on $2.5–5K/mo with Peak Seven / UltraWeb / OGK.
5. **"Boat-show readiness sprint — 6 weeks to PBIBS."** Marine / charter / yacht-service. Single deliverable, single deadline, reusable testimonial machine.
6. **Editorial wedge.** Publish "AI Implementation Field Notes — Palm Beach Edition" (newsletter / local podcast, one PBC SMB owner/week). Pitch a Boca Mag or Palm Beach Post column ("Your Stack Is Costing You a Snowbird Season"). Playbook B maps perfectly to this market's preference for understated authority.

**Cold-outreach sequencing:**
- **Q2–Q3 2026 (post-season, primary push):** FRLA monthly meetings; MIAPBC mixers; Greater Boca Raton Chamber events; 1909 pitch nights.
- **Q3 2026:** BNI Boca + Delray; LinkedIn outreach to Boca/Delray/WPB CPAs + law partners (Matt voice).
- **Q4 2026 (pre-season):** Direct mail + audio-letter to home services + hospitality framed "ready by snowbird season."
- **Always-on:** 1 byline/qtr (Boca Mag, Palm Beach Post) + 1 podcast appearance/mo.
