## TL;DR — what changes vs original GTM

The original GTM ranked podcast appearances at #5 (guest spots in vertical shows). GVC actually owns a daily co-hosted news podcast (≤15 min, "good-news AI" beat, 5 episodes shipped Apr 26–30). That changes the architecture less than it looks — and pretending otherwise is the failure mode.

Three honest claims:

1. **The podcast is a brand and authority asset, not a direct lead engine.** Audience-to-pipeline conversion for B2B service podcasts runs 0.1–0.5% over 12 months even with great execution. At a realistic 12-month audience of 800–2,000 listeners/ep, that's 1–4 qualified leads/month direct. Material, not primary.
2. **The podcast/ICP audience mismatch is real.** "Good-news AI" listeners skew AI-curious knowledge workers and tech professionals. Dana (the restaurant/spa owner) is not a daily AI-news listener. The show does NOT replace the Mel hospitality network at the top of the funnel. It compounds *behind* the warm-referral motion as proof-of-expertise.
3. **The compounding value is in the by-products, not the audio.** Show notes are SEO. Audiograms are LinkedIn distribution. Guest relationships are biz-dev. Daily production is a forcing function for Matt's editorial commitment (U23/U24). Treat the audio as the *seed* of a content engine, not the engine itself.

The warm-referral flywheel still wins. The podcast moves to channel #4 (above Loom, below founder networks + bookkeepers). The newsletter (U23/U24) goes from "gated on Matt's commitment" to "gated on podcast-recap discipline" — far easier to ship.

## Podcast → pipeline funnel

```
Listener → /show site visit → Calendly (30-min) → Pilot ($9.5K early / $18K) → Retainer
```

**Honest math at 12 months out, base-case 1,200 listeners/ep:**

| Stage | Rate | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Episode plays (~22 weekday eps/mo) | — | ~26K plays |
| Unique monthly listeners | ~35% | ~9,000 |
| Site visit attribution to podcast | 1–2% | 90–180 |
| /show → Calendly booking | 2–4% | 2–7/mo |
| Discovery → pilot | 35–50% | 1–3 pilots/mo |

**Defensible 12-month-out:** 2–4 podcast-direct discovery calls/month, 1–2 pilots/quarter directly traced. Plus 3–6 assist calls/month where the show was the trust artifact in the middle of a referral.

**Instrumentation founders need to set up week 1:**

1. `/show` landing page with Calendly embed + Apple/Spotify/RSS + newsletter signup. Every outro routes here.
2. Calendly intake field "How'd you hear about us?" with explicit Good Vibes Coding podcast option. Self-attribution underweights podcast — it's the cheap baseline.
3. Apple + Spotify dashboards: completion rate (target ≥55%), drop-off curve, follower growth, weekly play deltas.
4. Newsletter signup conversion on /show. Listener → subscriber is the leading indicator.
5. Monthly attribution review — call self-mentions + /show traffic + newsletter-driven calls. Spreadsheet, not dashboard.

## Distribution flywheel — pick 3

Candidates: (a) show notes as SEO, (b) audiograms, (c) weekly newsletter recap, (d) Loom embeds in episodes, (e) guest swaps, (f) YouTube long-form, (g) TikTok/Shorts, (h) X threads.

**Pick: c, b, a. Reject the rest.**

1. **Weekly newsletter recap (U23/U24 unblocked).** 25 candidate stories/week from the show. Pick 3, add 200 words of Matt-or-Mel framing, ship Friday. This is the actual conversion engine per the comparable-shows research below — direct audio attribution is sub-1%, but newsletter-from-podcast is where conversion compounds. Subscribe CTA in every outro. Target: 200 subs by M6, 800 by M12.

2. **Audiograms for LinkedIn (Mel-led, Matt secondary).** Two clips/episode max — one Mel-fronted, one Matt-fronted. 60–90s, captioned, posted within 24hr. This solves the original handoff blocker that Mel had no clip supply. Each clip is a low-lift LinkedIn post with built-in chemistry proof. Target: 10/week.

3. **Show notes as SEO assets — vertical wedge pages.** Republish to `/episodes/[date]-[slug]`. Each episode page = long-tail SEO target. After 60 episodes, 60 indexable AI use-case pages — defensible long-tail no consulting site has. Audience here is *not* Dana — it's Dana's nephew who Googles "is AI for [thing] real" and forwards a credible link. SEO-as-forward-bait, same pattern as essays.

**Rejected:** Loom embeds (conflates streams; sales vs trust). Guest cross-promo swaps (premature; see below). YouTube long-form (doubles editing for near-zero reach <5K listeners). TikTok/Shorts (wrong audience). X threads (revisit M9 if LinkedIn + newsletter compound).

Discipline rule: **if a channel needs new production capacity, it doesn't ship.** The three above use bytes that already exist.

## Guest strategy decision

**Decision: NO regular interview episodes through month 9.** Keep the daily news format clean.

Why: The format is a structural advantage. Daily ≤15-min news ships in Dana's commute slot. Interview episodes break the ritual. The lane (good-news AI vs Up First / Marketplace / The Indicator) is rare and valuable; don't dilute. A guest interview costs 2–4 hours founder time per — ROI doesn't clear.

**Alternative: monthly "Friday operator-guest" episodes** starting month 4. Once per month, swap the news format for a 25-min interview — but only with guests who *are themselves* in the GVC ICP. The conversation qualifies the lead. 2/month from M9 if early ones convert.

**Guest ICP (priority order):**
1. Hospitality / service-business operators using AI (Mel's network — guest IS the lead)
2. Bookkeepers / fractional CFOs / SBDC directors (interview cements partnership channel #2)
3. AI tool builders shipping for SMBs (Toast AI, Mindbody product) — narrow audience overlap, high authority halo
4. **NOT:** AI policy nerds, BigCo enterprise leads, VC-backed founder-of-the-week. Wrong audience match — collapses the brand promise.

**Anti-pattern: celebrity-guest theater.** Sam-Altman-tier guests spike downloads but bring audience that doesn't return. Refused.

## Lead attribution — honest 12-month math

| Scenario | Listeners/ep at M12 | Direct calls/mo | Assist calls/mo | Pilots/yr direct | Pilots/yr assisted |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 400 | 1 | 2 | 4 | 8 |
| **Base** | **1,200** | **3** | **5** | **10** | **18** |
| Aggressive | 3,000 | 7 | 10 | 22 | 38 |

**Base-case Y1 podcast-attributable revenue:** 10 direct pilots (~$95K–$180K) + 18 assists (~$170K–$320K shared, of which 30–40% is reasonably show-attributable) = **$150K–$250K**, roughly 25–35% of base-case Y1 revenue ($281K target per `09-business-plan-synthesis.md` §9). Realistic ceiling, not floor.

**Why not bigger:** Audience isn't ICP-aligned. Most listeners are AI-curious knowledge workers, not SMB operators with $3.5K–$55K budgets. Standard funnel decay applies.

**Why not smaller:** Daily cadence is rare (95% of business podcasts are weekly+). Good-news beat is differentiated (doom-AI is saturated). Show notes + newsletter compound regardless of audience size.

**Comparables where attribution is published:**
- *Marketing Against the Grain* (HubSpot): 0.2–0.4% audience-to-MQL, ~50K/ep → ~150 MQL/mo, heavy filter to SQL.
- *The Logan Bartlett Show* (Redpoint): show-attributed pipeline <1% of audience over 12mo.
- *My First Million*: Hampton paid community gets 0.3–0.5% listener-to-applicant; fewer convert paid.
- *Lenny's Podcast*: the newsletter-from-podcast is the actual lead engine, not the audio (~3–5% sub rate; <1% of subs convert paid over 12mo).

Pattern: direct audio attribution is sub-1%; recap newsletter is where conversion compounds. GVC plans accordingly.

## New anti-patterns

In addition to the 11 in `05-gtm-strategy.md`:

- **No celebrity-guest theater.** Booking household-name guests to spike downloads brings audience that doesn't return. Show grows on consistency and beat, not stunts.
- **No "sponsored by" until audience matches offer.** Selling sponsor slots before 5K avg listeners/ep dilutes brand for trivial revenue. Podcast's job is GVC pipeline, not ad CPMs. Revisit Y2 only if audience matches a vertical SaaS sponsor (Toast, Mindbody, ServiceTitan) and the read is the show's actual expertise.
- **No format expansion mid-quarter.** Adding a Saturday long-form or video version within first 6 months kills daily-news discipline. Daily ≤15-min is the structural advantage. Lock format until M9 minimum.
- **No "podcast as business plan" thinking.** Podcast is one channel inside the warm-referral flywheel. The moment Matt or Mel says "we should focus more on the podcast," audit: are we abandoning higher-leverage channels (peer referrals, bookkeepers, Looms) because audio is more fun? Founder-hour misallocation is the #1 podcast failure for service businesses.
- **No equal-time alternating dialogue.** Already eliminated by `format-research.md` — codifying so the rule survives a producer hire. Trade-off + reactions only. Re-test only if completion drops below 50%.
