## TL;DR — primary cold motion in one paragraph

With Mel's warm network smaller and less-moneyed than v1 assumed, GVC's primary motion shifts to **physical-presence + local-search dominance in two anchor markets** — Palm Beach County, FL and the Upper Delaware / Poconos corridor (Narrowsburg–Callicoon–Honesdale–Stroudsburg) — backed by a **monthly free workshop, quarterly direct-mail wave, and Chamber/BNI active membership in each market**. The owned daily podcast moves from #4 to #3 because in cold markets a daily-shipping show is the fastest path from "who are these people" to "they sound like they know what they're doing." Load-bearing TOFU = (1) in-person main-street + workshops and (2) local SEO / GBP / AI-Overview citations. Cold direct outreach is rationed against pre-warmed lists only (Chamber rosters, county filings, workshop attendees). **$20K MRR cold-only ramp: M8 base / M11–13 conservative**, with productized starter sprints ($4.5K/$6.5K/$7.5K) bridging cash while the recurring book builds.

## The cold-becomes-warm trust journey (per geography)

SMB-services buyers convert after 3–7 trust touches (Edelman 2024 / HubSpot 2025: median 5.4).

**Palm Beach (denser, faster, more skeptical):** (T1) Google "AI consultant Palm Beach" or meet Mel at PB Chamber breakfast → site/named humans/prices/show. (T2–3) Episode in car. Forward Loom to COO. Read essay. (T4) Monthly free workshop at WPB Mandel or Boca Innovation Campus — live consult signup at end. (T5) 30-min Calendly w/ Mel. (T6) One-page scope by Friday, no nag. (T7) Close on $4,500 Menu Sprint or $7,500 Site Refresh; retainer convo deferred to W2 of sprint. **Median PB: 3–6 weeks.** Urban density = no dawdle.

**Poconos (slower, warmer-by-default, longer window):** (T1) Meet Matt/Mel at Narrowsburg post office, Tusten Heritage Days, or Callicoon Market — or postcard, or Honesdale accountant mention. (T2–3) Site visit or one episode; local search shows GVC for "AI Catskills small business." (T4) Same farmers market next month, library workshop, or Chamber mixer. (T5) Direct-mail letter follow-up — signed, plain envelope. (T6) **They call or email — not Calendly first.** Rural buyers want phone before calendar; Mel takes it. (T7) Calendly the following week. (T8) Close on starter sprint or — common rural — $3,500 Diagnostic Audit first (smaller initial commitment). **Median Poconos: 6–14 weeks.** Close-rate at T6 ~55–65% vs PB ~35–45%.

**Implication:** PB demands the digital surface (skeptical urban buyers Google before they call). Poconos demands the physical surface (rural buyers trust people they've seen twice). Don't run one playbook for both.

## Local SEO + AI search visibility playbook

**Architecture: Service-Area Business, two GBPs** (non-overlapping service areas — Google permits). "Good Vibes Coding — Palm Beach" (PB + Broward) and "Good Vibes Coding — Upper Delaware / Poconos" (Sullivan NY, Wayne/Pike/Monroe PA). Both SAB, no storefront.

**GBP optimization:** named-humans photos, workshop photos, anonymized client dashboards, podcast cover. Categories: Business Management Consultant (primary), Marketing Consultant, Software Company. 8 self-seeded Q&As/profile. 2 posts/wk. Reviews routed to geo-matching profile.

**Local landing pages (M1–M3, 14 total):**
- `/palm-beach` → `/restaurant-ai`, `/hospitality-ai`, `/professional-services-ai`, `/medical-practice-ai`, `/west-palm-beach`, `/delray-beach`, `/boca-raton`
- `/poconos` → `/lodging-ai` (B&Bs, lake rentals), `/restaurant-ai`, `/narrowsburg`, `/honesdale`, `/stroudsburg`, `/hawley`

Each: 800–1,200 words, named humans, one Loom, local example, schema, pricing.

**Schema:** `LocalBusiness` w/ `areaServed`, `Service` w/ `offers.price`, `FAQPage` (5–8 voice-query Q&As), `Person` Matt/Mel, `PodcastSeries`+`PodcastEpisode`, `AggregateRating` once ≥5 reviews.

**Citations:** GBP, Apple Maps, Bing Places, FB, Yelp (PB only — dead in Poconos), BBB, Chamber directories. Skip Yellowpages/LocalIQ.

**AI Overview / ChatGPT / Perplexity targeting** — citation, not ranking, is the mechanism:
1. Publish `/llms.txt` and `/llms-full.txt` (table-stakes mid-2026).
2. FAQ block per page — question-shaped H2s, 60–120-word direct answers (the format AI Overview pulls).
3. Citations from mid-authority local sites (Palm Beach Post op-ed, River Reporter column, Pocono Record, Wayne Independent) — AI models weight local-news citations heavily for geo queries.
4. Structured data on /about, /matt, /mel, /show.
5. Show-notes pages = long-tail Q&A, natural training shape.
6. Reddit + HN thoughtful answers (not promotion) in r/smallbusiness, r/restaurateur, r/Poconos, r/SouthFlorida — heavy AI indexing.

## Workshop / office-hours strategy

**Format:** 75 min, free, 12–25 attendees, library or coworking. **Title:** "AI for Small Business: What Actually Works in 2026 (and What's Hype)" — buyer-protection framing.

**Run-of-show:** 0–5 Mel intro + round-robin ("I run X, biggest time-sink is Y"). 5–15 "SaaS layer is dying" thesis 90-sec, two real 2026 examples. 15–35 Matt builds live — given an attendee's actual problem, ship a working AI assistant in 15 min. 35–55 open Q&A. 55–70 small-group "what's your one AI question" — both circulate. 70–75 ONE CTA: "want a free 30-min look at your business? Sign up here. We'll do 3 next week." iPad opt-in.

**No deck pitching the firm. No logos. No closing sequence.** Slides content-only.

**Cadence:** 1/mo/geo M1–M3, ramp to 2/mo/geo M4+ if validated. **8/mo combined cap**.

**Logistics:** Free venues: WPB Mandel, Boca Raton PL, Western Sullivan PL (Narrowsburg/Jeffersonville), Wayne County PL (Honesdale), Eastern Monroe (Stroudsburg). Paid ($50–150): Boca Innovation Campus, FAU Tech Runway, NeXT Industries Stroudsburg, The Cooperage Honesdale. Materials $100/event (coffee, bagels, 1-pager handout, QR to /show + Calendly). Promotion via library calendar (free, locally indexed), Chamber newsletter, local FB groups, Nextdoor, GBP post. **Staffing:** Mel MCs, Matt builds. Both non-negotiable — operator+engineer duo IS the pitch.

**Benchmarks:** HubSpot 2024 SMB Workshop — live-CTA 15–25% vs post-event email 4–8%. Crowdcast 2024 / Riverside 2025 fractional-CTO precedents — workshop → paid 8–14% in 90 days. **GVC plan:** 20 × 18% × 35% close = **1.3 sprints/workshop**, $5.8K avg = ~$7.5K rev/workshop. **At 8/mo: ~$60K run-rate from workshops alone by M6** (lumpy M1–3).

**Risk:** workshops require both founders. If M2 podcast production overruns, compress workshops first — protect the build.

## Direct mail in cash-light markets

Older SMB owner avg age 57. Mail open ~80% vs B2B email ~22%. Poconos especially.

**List build (3–5 hr/geo):** county business filings (Sullivan NY, Wayne/Pike/Monroe PA assumed-name + DBA registries — public, free), Chamber directories (PB ~2,200 / Greater Pocono ~700 / Wayne ~400 / Sullivan ~350), Yelp+GBP scrapes 25-mi radius, county property records for B&B/lodging, Sullivan Catskills VA + Pocono Mtns VB rosters. Cleaned: ~3,000 PB + ~1,200 Poconos = 4,200; filter to "owner-operated $250K–$5M" → **~1,800 mailable**.

**Format A — postcard, $0.65 all-in (USPS Marketing Mail):** 6×9 white matte. Front handwritten-style headline: "We help small businesses build AI products instead of paying for SaaS that doesn't work." Back: 3-sentence body, Mel+Matt names+small B&W photos, URL `/poconos` or `/palm-beach`, phone, **no QR (USPS 2025: rural distrust ~3× urban)**. Hand-signed ballpoint (Mel does 50/hr w/ podcast playing).

**Format B — letter, $1.20 all-in:** plain white #10, real first-class stamp (not metered, not bulk indicia), hand-addressed Sundays, 280 words letterhead, both ink signatures. Lead: "We're two people in [Narrowsburg/Palm Beach] who help small businesses build custom AI tools instead of paying for SaaS that doesn't fit." Body: one named-vertical example, transparent pricing, no scarcity. Close: "If it's a fit, calendar's at [URL]. If not, throw this out — we won't hassle you."

**Cost / volume:**

| Vol | Postcard | Letter | Per-piece |
|---|---|---|---|
| 100 | $65 | $120 | $0.65 / $1.20 |
| 500 | $295 | $580 | $0.59 / $1.16 |
| 2,000 | $1,100 | $2,150 | $0.55 / $1.07 |

**Realistic response (DMA 2024 + USPS 2025):** generic postcard 0.3–0.8%; hand-signed + named-humans 1.0–2.2%; hand-addressed first-class signed letter 2.5–4.5%; **two-step series (postcard → letter T+3wk) 3.5–6%**; three-step adds <1pp — stop at two.

**Plan: two-step waves, one per geo per quarter** (Q1 W3 Poconos 600 / Q1 W6 PB 800 / Q2 W3 Poconos 600 new list / Q2 W6 PB 800 new list). Cost ~$1.0K Poconos, $1.4K PB per wave.

**Annual:** ~$10K spend → 170–340 responses → 70–140 booked discovery (40% conv) → 10–25 pilots → $50–150K revenue → **5–15× ROI**. Conservative; rural over-indexes.

**Anti-patterns:** glossy 4-color brochure, "limited time," QR codes, VistaPrint-template anything. Signal: *from a person*.

## Anti-SaaS cold-pitch language (real examples)

The thesis ("traditional SaaS is dead — we help SMBs build custom AI-native products that remove SaaS overhead") is the strongest cold-pitch frame GVC has. Names a pain (SaaS bills, SaaS that doesn't fit) + an attractive alternative (one custom thing). Has to land without sounding like an angry tech-bro.

**Email — pre-warmed (Chamber roster, name known):**
> Subject: Two humans in [Narrowsburg / Palm Beach] · 90 seconds
>
> Hi [Name] — Mel and I run a small AI shop in [Narrowsburg / West Palm]. We build custom AI tools for small businesses — usually replacing 2–3 SaaS subscriptions with one thing that does the job.
>
> Two recent examples: a B&B owner who replaced a $400/mo booking-and-review stack with a $40/mo custom assistant; a restaurant that stopped paying for menu-management software and now updates menu, allergens, and Google profile from one prompt.
>
> No deck, no demo. If you ever want to see what's possible for [specific business], we do free 30-min look-arounds. Calendar's at [URL]. If not, no follow-up. — Matt

**Loom voicemail (T+48hr to non-openers, 87 sec):** "Hey [Name], it's Matt at Good Vibes Coding. Sent an email but figured a 90-second video would land better. [Show landing page.] Two real examples — [5s each]. Calendar's right here. If not, won't bug you again."

**Door-pitch (market / main-street):** "Hi, I'm Mel. I run an AI shop with my partner Matt — we're in [Narrowsburg / on Worth Ave]. We help small businesses build custom AI tools instead of paying for SaaS that doesn't fit. Just wanted to introduce ourselves — here's a card. Free workshops at the library every month if you ever want to come by." [Hand card. Walk away. Don't pitch.]

**Postcard back:** "Two humans. We build AI for small businesses in [Sullivan County / Palm Beach County]. Most SaaS is overpriced and underfit — we make the custom thing that does what your business actually needs. $3,500 to look. $4,500 starter projects. We answer the phone. — Matt & Mel · goodvibescoding.com"

**Workshop opening:** "Most of you are probably here because you're paying for software that doesn't quite do what you want, and you've heard AI might fix that. The honest answer is: sometimes yes, sometimes no. We're going to spend the next 75 minutes figuring out which one applies to your business. No pitch."

**Discipline: never lead with "AI consulting." Lead with "we help you stop paying for SaaS that doesn't fit"** — buyer-side, problem-shaped, plain.

## Multi-market simultaneous operation reality

**Honest answer: 2 humans cannot run cold acquisition in two geos simultaneously without one suffering.** Cold-market presence depends on physical-touch density — can't fake from 1,400 miles.

- **A — Sequence (PB May–Sep, Poconos Sep–Mar):** dense saturation, clean attribution; but leaves Poconos dormant in peak-tourist summer (5 mo lost).
- **B — Split (Mel PB-resident, Matt Pocono-resident):** each market has a face; but workshops require both, podcast records remote half the week, the duo never appears together — brand promise weakens.
- **C — Rotation (recommended):** 2-wk PB / 2-wk Poconos. Workshop in each per rotation. Podcast records from anywhere (Riverside.fm). Both faces both markets, brand intact. Cost: $800–1,200/mo travel + 40 hr/mo travel time.

**Recommendation: Option C with PB lean (60/40 through M6)** — higher revenue density, faster cycles. Re-evaluate M6: if Poconos converts faster despite under-investment, flip the lean.

**First fractional Who — M4 hire: content + ops VA, $2.5–3K/mo, 15 hr/wk** (Manila or PHX market). Job: workshop scheduling + confirmations, direct-mail wave logistics, GBP posts (2/wk × 2), audiogram clipping + LinkedIn scheduling, newsletter assembly, Calendly + "how'd you hear" tracking. **BEFORE the audio editor (M3) and fractional ops/PM (M9)** in priority — multi-market generates this admin load specifically. Without it by M4, founders burn out M5–6. **Sales hire: never** — founder-owned in perpetuity.

## Anti-patterns refused

Extending the 11 in `05-gtm-strategy.md` and 5 in `11-gtm-v2-with-podcast.md`:

- **No "warm-network rescue" fantasy.** Bake any Mel warm-intros as upside, not plan.
- **No paid Google Local Service Ads.** "Get Quotes" UX violates Playbook B + forces auto-replies.
- **No QR codes on rural direct mail.** USPS 2025: rural distrust ~3× urban. URL + phone only.
- **No bulk-rate indicia on letters.** First-class stamp, hand-applied. Signal: from a person.
- **No "Free AI Audit" workshop title.** Educational only. "AI for Small Business: What Actually Works in 2026."
- **No selling from the workshop podium.** One CTA, end, iPad opt-in. Pitch is the workshop's quality.
- **No solo founder at events first 6 months.** The duo is the brand.
- **No identical content across geos.** PB pages: Worth Ave, hospitality density. Poconos: lake rentals, Tusten Heritage Days. Templated copy fails AI Overview citation tests.
- **No third-geography talk before M9.** Two is already at the edge.
- **No Chamber sponsor tiers above base member in Y1.** Showing up beats sponsoring.
- **No live-build IP given away without capture.** Screen-record w/ consent → Loom + episode + landing-page proof.
- **No cold-cold LinkedIn DM blasts.** Channel #11 = pre-warmed only (Chamber roster, workshop attendees, mail responders).

|---|---|---|---|
