This document supersedes `18-business-plan-synthesis-v2.md`. It folds in the v3 research bundle (`19`–`22`) and applies four founder-driven decisions:

1. **The "traditional SaaS is dead" thesis is the brand spine.** Every offering replaces a SaaS the customer is renting.
2. **WikiFold stays free.** Used as proof-of-capability, not productized as a SaaS.
3. **No warm-network primary motion.** Mel's hospitality network is small and cash-light. Plan assumes cold acquisition.
4. **Two geographies:** Palm Beach County FL + the Narrowsburg/Poconos region (Sullivan NY + Wayne/Pike/Monroe PA).
5. **$20K MRR target as fast as honestly possible.**

Read this end-to-end. §13 has the diff vs v2 if you've already read v2.

## 1. Executive summary (one page)

**The bet.** Traditional SaaS is dying for small businesses. Every SMB is paying $200–$800/month for a stack of cookie-cutter software they barely use. AI lets a 2-person shop build custom replacements faster and cheaper than ever. **GVC = the local AI shop that replaces your SaaS rent with code you own.**

**The path.** Stay 2-person + productized. Three small-ticket cold-converting starters that ladder to recurring retainers. Cold acquisition driven by local SEO + an in-person workshop circuit + an owned podcast/newsletter. No SaaS spinoff. No multi-tier subscriptions. No outside capital.

**The math.** $20K MRR is **honestly month 9–10 base case** in cold-only motion (month 7–8 upside if cold paid ads convert AI Front-Desk at 4–5%). Year-1 total revenue ~$510K base case (workshop-driven sprint volume layers on top of recurring). Year-3 ceiling ~$1.5M, 5–6 person firm, ~$400K combined founder draw. **No outside capital required in any scenario.**

**The risk.** Cold acquisition is slower-starting than warm. The first 60 days produce mostly conversations, not invoices. Founders need to commit to physical presence in both geographies — neither can be served from Florida alone. Skipping the in-person infrastructure (workshops, Chamber sponsorships, direct mail in the Poconos) collapses the model.

**Warm referrals are upside, not plan.** Any inbound from Mel's network or Matt's eng peers gets booked. Nothing in the plan depends on it.

### The trust journey is geography-specific

- **Palm Beach:** 5 touches / 3–6 weeks. Most prospects book Calendly cold within the trust window.
- **Poconos:** 7–8 touches / 6–14 weeks. **Rural buyers call before they Calendly.** Phone the cold-pitch language for this market.

### Workshop circuit as the load-bearing channel

- Format: 75-minute library / Chamber / coworking workshop, "AI for Small Business — One Hour."
- Cadence: 2/month per geography (4/month total) by M3.
- Conversion target: 30 attendees → 3–4 booked 30-min individual consults → 1–2 paid sprints per workshop = **~$7.5K average revenue/workshop**.
- Run-rate by M6: **~$60K/month from workshops alone** if hit cadence and conversion benchmarks.

### Multi-market reality

- **2-week Palm Beach / 2-week Poconos rotation**, 60/40 PB lean through M6.
- **3 residency weeks/year in Poconos** non-negotiable: February (Chamber circuit + budget-reset window), Memorial Day, mid-September.
- **Cold email scales to zero in the Poconos market.** Physical presence is the only way in. Florida-based delivery works only with these residency commitments.
- **Part-time local Poconos field rep at $1,500/mo from month 12.** Budget the line.

### Cold-pitch language (use, don't translate)

Email subject (Palm Beach restaurant): *"Quick question about the $400/mo your restaurant spends on Yext + Birdeye + OpenMenu"*

Door-pitch opener (Poconos B&B): *"You don't know me. I'm Matt. I noticed your B&B is paying [Booking platform] 14% of every direct booking. We're a 2-person shop in [town] — we replace the stack with code you own, one fixed fee. Got 90 seconds?"*

Workshop opening line: *"This is not an AI consulting pitch. This is one hour on the three pieces of software you're paying for that AI can replace by Friday — and the one you should keep."*

**Never lead with "AI consulting."** Lead with "we help you stop paying for SaaS that doesn't fit."

## 5. The two geographies (pricing differentiated)

Sources: `19-south-florida-market.md` + `20-poconos-upstate-market.md`.

### Palm Beach County (West Palm, Palm Beach, Boca, Delray, Wellington, Jupiter, Boynton)

- **~45–50K employer firms** across the 9 target cities.
- **Cohort priority:** hospitality/F&B → home services → professional services → med spas / wellness → marine → real estate (real estate is referral-only, post-MIAMI/RWorld merger).
- **Best buying window:** May–July (post-snowbird debrief, planning next-season investments). Worst: Nov–Apr (peak season eats attention).
- **Pricing tier:** standard portfolio (3 starters in §3, retainers $250–$500/mo).
- **Competitive vacuum:** 8 named local agencies sell SaaS + paid media. None pitch "AI-native, replaces SaaS." ~30–50 solo "AI consultants" on LinkedIn are mostly ChatGPT trainers without engineering depth. **GVC's wedge is the pitch nobody else is making.**
- **Anchor cold-channel stack:** Greater Boca Raton Chamber (1,500 members, 300+ events/yr), FRLA Palm Beach Chapter, MIAPBC, BNI Boca/Delray/WPB, 1909 coworking.

### Narrowsburg / Poconos region (Sullivan NY + Wayne/Pike/Monroe/Carbon PA)

- **~422,650 residents, $8.2B/yr visitor spending** — 150% of Palm Beach's tourism-spend-per-capita despite 1/4 the population.
- **Connectivity gates:** Wayne County PA has ~33% of residents with no internet. Sullivan ConnectALL fiber rollout begins Liberty May 2026. **Town cores work for cloud delivery; outlying farms don't.** Don't sell to a customer with bad internet.
- **Pricing must drop ~30%:** retainer floor $750–$1,200/mo for year-round locals; $1,500–$2,500/mo only for weekender-owned hospitality (Hotel Fauchère class). **"Ouch" line for year-round operators is ~$300/mo total software spend.**
- **Best buying window:** **Feb 1 – Apr 15 off-season.** Owners are busy May–Oct. Cold-outreach push lands in late winter when budgets reset.
- **Sharper cold-pitch wedges with named first-call lists** (per `20`):
  - **B&B Stack Consolidator** — $7,500 + $850/mo (use AFTER physical-presence trust is built; not a cold-first offer)
  - **Wedding-Venue Inquiry System** — $6,500 + $600/mo (Catskills wedding boom, post-trust offer)
  - **Outfitter Booking + Waiver Modernizer** — $5,500 + $700/mo (Delaware River outfitters, post-trust)
  - **Restaurant Reviews + Menu Sprint** — $3,500 flat
- **Competitive vacuum:** No agency in the corridor (LVL UP, EZ Marketing, Inception Digital Pro, WRIS, Service Rankers) positions around AI / SaaS-replacement. They all run 2010-vintage SEO + web. **The integrator role is empty.**

### How the pricing tiers reconcile

- **Cold-first conversation (month 1–3):** sell the §3 cold-conversion starters ($750 / $1,500 / $1,800).
- **After 1–2 reference customers in a town and physical-presence trust (month 3+):** the Poconos-named premium offers ($3,500–$7,500 sprints, $600–$850/mo retainers) become realistic.
- **Palm Beach pricing stays at the standard tier through year 1.** Premium ticket comes from the Restaurant Full Stack bundle ($3,500 + $500/mo) at month 3+.

## 8. The honest $20K MRR math

### Base case: $20K MRR by **month 9–10**

- Workshops at 2/geography/month from M3 → ~4 workshops/month × ~3 sprints/workshop = ~12 sprints/month
- Cold-conversion price mix: 60% AI Visibility ($750 + $250/mo), 30% Menu+Reviews Lite ($1,500 + $300/mo), 10% AI Front-Desk ($1,800 + $200/mo built-in)
- Sprint→retainer conversion: 50% (cold motion baseline)
- ~6 retainers/month landing → ~$1.5K MRR/month new
- Add Restaurant Full Stack from M3 (slow at first, building): adds ~$500/mo MRR/month new from M5+
- **MRR projection:** M3 ~$2K, M5 ~$8K, M7 ~$13K, M9 ~$18K, **M10 ~$21K** ✓

### Upside case: $20K MRR by **month 7–8**

Triggered if any of these hit:
- Cold paid ads convert AI Front-Desk at AI-receptionist-SaaS benchmarks (4–5%) — adds ~$5K/mo MRR from month 5.
- Workshop conversion runs above 3 sprints/workshop (4+).
- The Restaurant Full Stack bundle takes off in Palm Beach faster than expected.
- A second part-time hire (junior implementer) lands at month 6 to handle delivery throughput.

### Conservative case: $20K MRR by **month 11–13**

Triggered if any of these breaks:
- Workshop attendance below 15 / under 2 sprints/workshop conversion.
- Direct mail under 0.5% response rate.
- Multi-market rotation slips and Poconos pipeline stalls.
- Any geography-fit/family event (founder bandwidth issue).

### Year-1 total revenue (base case): **~$510K**

Higher than the v2 base case ($281K) **because workshop-driven sprint volume layers on top of recurring revenue.** The trade is that cash flow is more lumpy and less front-loaded.
