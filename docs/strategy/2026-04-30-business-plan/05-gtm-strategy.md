## TL;DR — primary motion in one sentence

GVC's go-to-market is a warm-referral flywheel: Melissa's hospitality network and Matt's engineering peers plus a thin layer of editorial proof (one Matt essay/month, one Loom sample build/month) feed a single CTA — book a 30-min Calendly with a named human — and we partner with bookkeepers, accountants, and Chamber/SBDC people who already sit across Dana's desk to keep the top of funnel full without ever cold-prospecting.

## ICP & buyer journey (Dana refined)

**Dana is not browsing AI consulting sites.** She is doing payroll at 10pm. Her funnel is shorter than B2B SaaS funnels assume — she goes from "I should look into this" to a booked call in 48 hours, or never. The job of GTM is to be the obvious answer at the moment of trigger.

**Where she actually is, ranked by reachability:**

1. **Owner peer rooms.** BNI chapters (8am Tuesdays, ~30 owners/chapter, every metro), EO Forum (8-person monthly groups, $400K+ revenue gate), Vistage (peer advisory groups, ~$1k/mo), local Chamber of Commerce monthly mixers, industry-specific Slacks (Restaurant Owners United, Spa Industry Association, Independent Insurance Agents Slack). She trusts the table she sits at.
2. **Her bookkeeper / accountant / fractional CFO.** The single most influential third party in her business. They see every SaaS invoice. When they say "you should talk to these people," she calls within a week.
3. **Her web/IT person.** Already trusted with logins. Often the first person she asks "is this AI thing real?" Half the time they're the ones forwarding our site.
4. **Industry trade Facebook groups.** Restaurant Owners (110k members), Salon Owners (80k+), Independent Hotel Owners. High noise, but the mod-vetted recommendations land.
5. **One podcast appearance in her vertical.** Restaurant Strategy with Chip Klose, The Profitable Salon, Smart Hotelier — single-episode appearance compounds for 6+ months because podcasts live in her phone during prep, drive time, and inventory counts.
6. **Google / GBP at the moment of intent.** "AI consultant West Palm Beach," "AI for restaurants south florida," "Zapier alternative." Low volume but high intent.

**What actually triggers a booked call** (not a browse):

- **A peer at Tuesday BNI says: "Talk to these two — they actually built me the thing the SaaS was supposed to."** This is the dominant motion. Everything else is in service of producing this sentence.
- **Her bookkeeper texts the GVC link with "the SaaS audit thing might be worth a call."**
- **She watches one 4-minute Loom sample build (U17/U20) where a named team member's Tuesday looks different by the end.** The Loom does what a deck cannot: shows a human she could be, doing work she does, with the awkward parts left in.
- **An essay lands in her inbox via a forward — not a subscribe — that names her exact 10pm reconciliation moment.** Editorial works as forward-bait, not subscriber-funnel.

**What actively repels her** (operational anti-list, not just brand):

- A "Book a Free AI Audit" pop-up. She has had eleven of those and they were all sales calls.
- A chatbot in the corner. Tool fatigue tell #1.
- A logo wall of enterprises she's never met. She is not Wayfair.
- A "schedule a 60-minute discovery call." 30 minutes max. Calendly only.

**Total realistic monthly lead volume at month 9–12:** 22–35 booked discovery calls/month. At founder capacity (4–6 active engagements at a time, 90-day average engagement = 16–24 engagements/year), this is the right order of magnitude — generates demand surplus that lets us be selective and triggers the "hire a Who" conversation in Q3–Q4.

## Sales motion & conversion benchmarks

**Stage 1 — 30-min discovery (Calendly).** Mel takes hospitality/service. Matt takes eng-adjacent and SaaS-stack-replacement. First 10 minutes: what's broken. Minutes 10–20: is AI actually the answer (we will say no ~25% of the time and that is the point). Minutes 20–30: if yes, scope a $X two-week pilot or a $Y audit. Never sell on the first call. End with: "We'll send you a one-page scope by Friday, no pressure."

**Stage 2 — Pilot or Audit.** Two-week fixed-scope, fixed-price. One shipped artifact (an automation, an audit doc, a sample build). Counter-programs the 90-day consulting default. Pilot outcome: a Loom-able win or an honest "this isn't worth continuing" — both build trust.

**Stage 3 — Retainer or Project.** Monthly retainer (range published per U18) or a defined build. Paper signed inside week 3 of pilot if going to ongoing.

**Conversion targets** (founder-honest, not VC-deck math):

| Step | Target rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Site visit → discovery booked | 2–4% | High for plain-language sites; hero + pricing + Loom drives this |
| Discovery → pilot/audit sold | 35–50% | Honest disqualification cuts this from typical 60% but raises pilot→retainer |
| Pilot → ongoing retainer or follow-on project | 55–70% | Depends entirely on pilot quality; this is the actual product |
| Closed-customer → referral within 6mo | 40–60% | The flywheel — if this is below 40%, the engagement quality failed |

**Cadence.** Same-day Calendly confirmation with a 90-second Mel-or-Matt Loom intro ("here's what to expect, here's what we'll ask"). One-page scope inside 48hr of call. One follow-up at day 7 if no response, then drop to a quarterly check-in. **No drip sequence.** No "circling back." Playbook B.

**Honest call lengths.** Discovery 30min hard cap. Scope-review 30min. Pilot kickoff 45min. Weekly pilot check-in 20min Loom (async preferred). Total founder time per closed engagement pre-revenue: ~3 hours.

## First 90 days — week by week

**Week 1 (Apr 30 – May 6).** Mel writes a list of 25 named hospitality contacts (NYC + SoFL). Matt writes a list of 15 named engineering peers + 5 prior org contacts. Both send a personal note — not a pitch — to 8 people each: "We're doing this thing now, here's the link, who comes to mind?" That's 16 warm asks in week 1. Matt ships first 4-min Loom sample build (U17/U20 pattern). Calendly + 30-min slot live. Pricing ranges (U18) published. Disqualification block (U15) published.

**Week 2.** Mel emails 8 more from her list. Matt drafts essay #1 ("The 10pm reconciliation"). Both set up 3 bookkeeper/accountant coffees for week 3–4 from existing contacts. Mel joins one BNI visitor day in West Palm. GBP claimed and filled.

**Week 3.** Essay #1 ships. Matt LinkedIn-posts the pull-quote with a link, not the whole thing. Mel does 3 bookkeeper coffees. First Loom now has 2 weeks of distribution; track who watched. First discovery call happens (target: 1–2 calls in week 3). Outreach to 3 podcasts in hospitality + 2 in eng-adjacent for late-Q2 booking.

**Week 4.** Mel emails final 9 from her list. Matt drafts second Loom. Bookkeeper partnerships: ask the 3 from week 3 if there's one client they'd intro. SBDC outreach — email regional director, ask about a "free AI office hours" slot. Target end-of-month: 4 discovery calls completed, 1 pilot sold.

**Weeks 5–6.** Loom #2 ships. First pilot kickoff. Essay #2 drafted. Mel attends one EO chapter event as a guest. Apply to speak at one Chamber lunch (typical lead time: 6–10 weeks). Matt does first podcast recording.

**Weeks 7–8.** Pilot delivery + Loom-able win. Essay #2 ships. First referral from the bookkeeper partnership lands (best case). Discovery cadence target: 6–8 calls/month run-rate. Mel onboarded to LinkedIn — three posts: who she is, what she's seen on a Friday dinner rush, why an operator and an engineer is the unlock.

**Weeks 9–10.** Pilot wraps. Convert to retainer or to one follow-on project. Case study (named, with the team member's quote, not the owner's). Loom #3 — this one is the actual case study. Essay #3 ships. Apply to 2 more podcasts; first podcast airs.

**Weeks 11–12.** Quarterly review. Hard count: how many calls, how many pilots, how many ongoing. Re-rank channels by actual lead source. Decide whether to commit to the newsletter (gated on Matt's reality check from handoff). Set Q2 plan.

**90-day exit metrics (target):** 18–24 discovery calls completed; 6–9 pilots sold; 3–5 ongoing engagements; 3 essays + 3 Looms shipped; 2 podcasts recorded (1 aired); 4 active bookkeeper/accountant partnerships; 1 SBDC or Chamber slot booked; ≥1 referral that did not come from a founder's direct list.

## First 12 months — quarterly milestones

**Q1 (May–Jul 2026) — Prove the warm motion.** Network outreach + first Looms + first essays + bookkeeper partnerships. Goal: prove that warm asks + editorial proof produces 6+ calls/month and 50%+ discovery→pilot. Exit signal: 3–5 ongoing engagements, 1 unsolicited referral.

**Q2 (Aug–Oct 2026) — Compound the proof.** Add podcast circuit (1/mo), expand to 2 SBDC or Chamber slots/quarter, ship first WikiFold-pattern free tool as a credibility asset. Mel's LinkedIn ramps. First "we said no" essay (the disqualification block in long form). Goal: 12–15 discovery calls/month, lead source diversifying away from founder direct lists. Exit signal: 50% of new calls cite "I heard from / saw / a friend sent" — not "Matt and I worked together."

**Q3 (Nov 2026–Jan 2027) — Operationalize.** Demand exceeds founder capacity. Decide: cap engagements (named small team stays at 2) or hire fractional Who. Default per positioning brief §8: cap at 4–6 active engagements, raise prices, lengthen waitlist, hire **fractional ops/coordinator first** (handles scoping calls, follow-ups, scheduling — frees Matt+Mel for delivery and judgment). Not a sales hire. Sales is the founders' job in perpetuity per Playbook B. Exit signal: a 4-week waitlist exists.

**Q4 (Feb–Apr 2027) — Productize the inbound.** First small-named-team hire (engineer #2 or operator #2, depending on pipeline mix). Newsletter at 500+ subs if Matt committed in Q1. Two podcast recurring relationships (guest 2x/yr). One vertical fully owned — restaurants/hospitality is the obvious bet given Mel. Exit signal: revenue run-rate $750k–$1.5M, pipeline 6+ weeks deep, churn <15%.

## Anti-patterns we refuse

- **Cold email / cold LinkedIn DMs.** Violates Playbook B, and Dana has been burned by eleven of them. Warm asks only.
- **Paid traffic before brand is sharp.** Paid converts brand into volume; with a soft brand it converts dollars into noise. Revisit Q4 if at all.
- **Generic "AI consulting" SEO grind.** "AI consultant near me" is contested by every Make.com hobbyist. We rank for "AI for restaurants south florida" and "replace your SaaS stack" — narrow, defensible, vertical+wedge.
- **RFP responses.** Anyone running a formal RFP is shopping for a Big Consulting deck. Decline politely.
- **Vendor portal listings (G2, Capterra, Clutch).** Wrong buyer, wrong tone, requires logo theater.
- **"Free AI audit" as a lead magnet.** Every competitor has it. Becomes a sales call. We do paid 2-week pilots; the audit is the deliverable, not the bait.
- **Webinars.** Funnel theater. Replace with Looms (async) and podcast guesting (someone else's audience, no production lift).
- **"Done-for-you" language anywhere.** We work *with* you. Per competitive scan §8.
- **Three-verb headlines.** "Identify. Build. Deploy." is the Big Consulting tell.
- **Engagements whose business case requires firing someone.** Hard rule per positioning brief §4.
- **12-month minimums.** Two-week pilot, then monthly retainer, cancel anytime. Lower commitment is the SMB-friendly move.

## Capacity math — when demand exceeds supply

**Inputs.**
- Founder capacity: 4–6 active engagements per positioning brief §8 (hard cap on "two humans" promise).
- Average engagement length: 90 days (pilot 2 weeks + retainer 60–75 days; some longer).
- Engagement throughput: 16–24 closed engagements/year at full velocity.
- Conversion: ~3% site→discovery, ~40% discovery→pilot, ~60% pilot→ongoing.

**Funnel math at month 9–12 steady state.**
- 25–30 discovery calls/month → 10–12 pilots/month → 6–7 ongoing engagements/month.
- At 90-day avg engagement length, 6–7 new ongoing/month means ~18–21 active at any time.
- Founder cap = 4–6 active. **Demand exceeds supply by 3–4x by month 9.**

**Trigger: hire a fractional Who.**
- **First hire (Q3 2026):** fractional ops/coordinator, ~$3–5k/mo. Handles scoping doc generation, Calendly ops, follow-up cadence, partnership pipeline, podcast booking. Frees ~10–15hr/wk per founder. Buys ~2 additional engagement slots without breaking the "two humans" promise (the founders still do every call and every build).
- **Second hire (Q4 2026 / Q1 2027):** fractional engineer or operator, project-based at first. Scales the small-named-team to 3–4 humans without breaking Playbook B (everyone is named on the site, everyone does real work).
- **Sales hire: never.** Sales is founder-owned in perpetuity. The moment a non-founder pitches, the brand collapses.

**Earlier trigger if it happens.** If by week 8 we hit 4 active engagements *and* the pipeline has 8+ qualified discovery calls booked, accelerate the fractional ops hire to Q2 instead of Q3. Pipeline is not the constraint — founder hours are.

**Lead-to-cash time.** Discovery → pilot signed: median 9 days (range 3–21). Pilot signed → first invoice cleared: 7 days. Pilot kickoff → retainer signed: 14 days. Total cold-warm-lead-to-recurring-revenue: ~30–45 days. This is the fastest motion in SMB consulting at this price band — a structural advantage that justifies the warm-only top of funnel.
