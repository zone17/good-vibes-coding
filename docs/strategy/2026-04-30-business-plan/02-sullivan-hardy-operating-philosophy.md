This translates the Sullivan/Hardy stack — *10x Is Easier Than 2x*, *Who Not How*, *Gap and Gain*, Unique Ability, Self-Multiplying Company, ABC Model — into GVC's operating system. It is load-bearing: revenue model, GTM, and financial plan all derive from here. Underlying thesis: ~33M US SMBs need AI deployed, not built (Cuban, Apr 21 2026). Value is in implementation judgment and operator credibility — both compounding, both what GVC has.

## 1. The 10x trajectory (vs the 2x grind)

Sullivan, paraphrased: 2x is linear and pulls you into work you dislike. 10x is non-linear, requires *eliminating* 80% of current activity, and pulls you toward the smaller, sharper version of the work only you can do. 10x is easier because it forces choices 2x lets you avoid.

**The 2x grind we refuse.** Six clients/month at $5–10k each. Founders personally scoping, building, training, supporting every engagement. Revenue grows linearly with hours; a sick week is a revenue gap. By month 18 both founders are senior implementation engineers with no time to write or think. Caps around $600–900k/yr, two exhausted owners, no transferable asset.

**The 10x version we are building.** ~30 engagements/year at $35–60k average plus ~$15k/mo recurring retainer. Three productized packages (Replace-the-SaaS-Stack Audit, 90-Day Augmentation Build, Ongoing Ops Partner). A fractional team of three Whos (§3) handling implementation, ops, content. Founders do only what they are uniquely best at. Revenue ~$1.2–1.8M with ~50% to founders, ~25–35% to Whos, ~15–20% to systems/content. Founder hours: 25–30/week, not 60.

**Substitutions that make 10x cheaper than 2x:**
- *Bespoke discovery* → published $2.5k Audit, 10 days, fixed scope. Kills 4–6 hrs/week of free scoping calls.
- *From-scratch builds* → playbook + reusable library (n8n, prompts, eval harnesses) — each build is ~60% configuration.
- *Founder-delivered training* → Loom onboarding course. Record once, play forever.
- *"Two engineers" builds* → operator + engineer as the unit; fractional implementer does heavier config under Matt's review.
- *Lead gen by sales* → editorial + Melissa's hospitality network. We refuse outbound.

The bet: this category supports $35–60k productized engagements because Big Consulting won't go below $250k, SaaS doesn't deploy, and every owner-operator we've talked to has $30k+ of annual SaaS waste on the table. If pricing power doesn't hold we revisit; the 2x grind is always available as fallback and we don't hedge for it.

## 2. Founder Unique Abilities

Sullivan's Unique Ability test, paraphrased: an activity qualifies when (1) you are objectively excellent at it, (2) it energizes you, (3) you keep getting better, (4) it produces disproportionate value. Fail 2 or 3, it belongs to a Who.

### Matt

**Inside.** The *judgment layer* of every deployment — what's agentic, what's deterministic, what's human-in-the-loop, what evals matter. 25 years of engineering compresses into 30-minute calls that take a typical AI consultant a week. Building the playbook and reusable IP — prompt libraries, eval harnesses, n8n/Temporal/Vercel components — pays back across every future engagement. Editorial / long-form — essays, "what we won't do," WikiFold. Final code review on every active engagement (~1 hr each).

**Outside (must move to Whos).** Configuring flows and wiring integrations; bug-fix support; status updates; prospecting calls with leads who haven't read the disqualification block; bookkeeping and contract admin.

### Melissa

**Inside.** First 30-minute call with every prospect — does the owner pass the smell test, is the problem AI-shaped, will the team adopt. 20 years on floor teams means she sees adoption risk in three questions. Owner-operator credibility on the front of the business — homepage, podcasts, hospitality referrals, the "yes she's made payroll on a slow week" trust signal. GVC's most leveraged marketing input. Designing the human side of every deployment — who owns it client-side, rollout sequence, change-management landmines — the most underweighted skill in AI consulting and why most augmentation deployments fail. Hospitality vertical thought leadership — essays, talks, anchor podcast.

**Outside (must move to Whos).** PM mechanics (Notion boards, status docs, calendars); onboarding logistics (credentials, doc collection); social/newsletter cadence; HR/contractor admin.

### What falls *outside* both

Where the 80% reduction lives: all low-judgment configuration; all recurring-cadence ops (status emails, scheduling, invoicing, distribution); all Tier-1 support until escalation; all inbound triage.

Sullivan's heuristic, paraphrased: if a competent contractor at $50–125/hr could do it, the founder doing it is destroying value. Both founders keep a 30-day A/B/C log — A (energizing, only-I), B (drains me, someone else could), C (shouldn't be doing). Target by day 90: ≥70% of founder hours in A.

## 3. The first 3 Whos to hire (next 12 months)

Sullivan's "Who Not How," paraphrased: the wrong question is "how do I do this?" — the right one is "who has already done this and can do it for me?" Cost is rarely the binding constraint; willingness to give up control of the *how* is. We sequence by leverage.

### Who #1 — Fractional Implementation Engineer (months 0–3)

- **Owns.** Configuring n8n / Temporal / Make flows from Matt's spec. Wiring integrations. Building the deterministic 70% of every engagement. Running the eval harness. Tier-2 support.
- **Replaces.** ~15 hrs/week of Matt's lowest-judgment build time. Highest-ROI hire — Matt is the bottleneck on every active engagement and ~60% of his current build hours sit below his Unique Ability line.
- **Shape.** Fractional, 20–25 hrs/week, 1099, $90–125/hr. We want elastic capacity, not a full-time seat.
- **Where to find.** Ex-staff engineers from Matt's prior orgs who went independent post-2024 layoffs. Backup: senior automation engineers in n8n / Make communities who've shipped agentic systems. Disqualify portfolios that are "I built a chatbot."

### Who #2 — Operations / Client Success Lead (months 3–6)

- **Owns.** Every recurring-cadence touch — onboarding logistics, status emails, weekly check-ins, credentials, invoicing, contract admin, project boards, Tier-1 client interface, post-engagement asset hand-back.
- **Replaces.** ~10 hrs/week of Melissa's coordination + ~5 hrs/week of Matt's admin. More importantly, *enables* Melissa to spend those hours on front-of-business credibility work that moves top-of-funnel.
- **Shape.** Fractional → part-time, 25–30 hrs/week, $35–55/hr.
- **Where to find.** Hospitality ops alumni from Melissa's network — someone who's run a 20-person FOH or BOH, hates spreadsheets, curious about AI. *Not* a tech-agency PM. Hospitality DNA is the point: pattern-matches to clients, not engineers.

### Who #3 — Editorial / Content Producer (months 6–12)

- **Owns.** Turning founder talk-tracks into shipped artifacts — essays, podcasts, Looms, case studies, distribution. Both founders write well; neither has time to be a *publisher*.
- **Replaces.** ~6 hrs/week of Matt's writing + ~4 hrs/week of Melissa's content prep. More importantly, replaces the missing weeks where client work crowded everything out. Editorial cadence compounds the brand; gaps kill it.
- **Shape.** Fractional, 10–15 hrs/week, $75–125/hr. One named senior freelancer, not an agency.
- **Where to find.** Independent editors who've shaped technical-but-readable founder voices — ex-newsletter editors, ex-Substack producers, ex-trade-press journalists. Disqualify anyone pitching "SEO content."

**NOT hiring in year 1.** A salesperson (funnel is editorial + Melissa's network; a sales seat corrupts the voice). A second senior engineer at Matt's level (model assumes he is the irreplaceable judgment layer — replicating him is year 3+, if ever). A full-time bookkeeper (fractional at <$1k/mo).

## 4. Gap-and-Gain measurement cadence

Sullivan/Hardy, paraphrased: the *Gap* is distance between where you are and an idealized future; standing in it drains energy because the ideal moves every time you approach it. The *Gain* is distance between where you are and where you actually started. Measuring backward against the starting point produces durable confidence. For two founders coming off years of W-2 grind the failure mode is obvious: $80k MRR feels broke if the comparison is an imagined $300k. The cadence is built to prevent that.

**Weekly (Friday, 30 min).** Three questions in order: (1) *What shipped this week that wasn't shipping a year ago?* — Gain question, before any planning. (2) *What's in the A-column for next week?* (3) *What B/C work needs to move to a Who?* — every recurring B/C item is a hiring signal. We do not start the week with "what's behind?"

**Monthly (last Friday, 90 min).** Backwards dashboard: revenue this month vs. last; vs. 12 months ago; engagements shipped; founder hours actually worked (target ≤30/wk each); % founder hours in A-column; retainer MRR. *Then* forward planning. Each month write three "wins we'd have killed for a year ago" before any problem-solving.

**Quarterly (half-day offsite, not at home).** 12-week look-back / look-forward. Look-back: what did we 2x or 10x against same quarter last year? What dropped from C? Who got promoted to a Who? Look-forward: single 10x bet for next quarter (one, not three); what can be productized that we still bespoke. One-page plan, not a deck.

**Annual (mid-December, two-day offsite).** Self-multiplying audit (§5). Every system, asset, Who reviewed for whether it's compounding or merely surviving.

**Single emotional rule.** No founder review starts with "we should be further ahead." That sentence is banned. If it shows up, the meeting restarts from the Gain question.

## 5. Self-Multiplying Company architecture

Sullivan, paraphrased: a company is self-multiplying when ability to deliver value grows without proportional growth in founder hours. Mechanism: *systems, IP, and Whos* substituting for founder time. A consulting business without all three is a job, not a company.

**Systems (operating layer).**
- Notion engagement OS: every engagement runs the same template — kickoff, audit, build sprints, eval, handoff, retainer transition. Who #2 owns it.
- Loom library: founder-recorded explainers of every recurring concept (evals, "human-in-the-loop," how the retainer works, week-1 expectations). Record once, play forever.
- Weekly automated metrics rollup: revenue, hours, A-column %, retainer MRR — from Stripe + a time-tracker, in Slack every Friday before founder review.
- Standing eval harness on every shipped automation: tells us in 30 minutes whether deployed systems have degraded. Without this, retainer revenue is a liability.

**IP (compounding layer).**
- Published GVC Implementation Playbook — initially internal, progressively externalized as essays + a gated PDF. Sales asset and hiring filter ("read this; if you disagree we won't be a fit").
- Reusable component library — n8n / Temporal / Vercel blocks, prompt patterns, eval templates, runbooks. Owned by Matt, extended by Who #1.
- WikiFold and future open-source tools as evidence assets, not products.
- Case-study library: 6–10 published before/after deployments by month 18, each both sales asset and teaching artifact.

**Productized engagements (package layer).** Three SKUs, defined in §1: Replace-the-SaaS-Stack Audit, 90-Day Augmentation Build, Ongoing Ops Partner. Bespoke is forbidden.

Architecture is finished when a new engagement can be sold by Melissa, scoped from the playbook, built ~60% by Who #1 from templates, PM'd by Who #2 against the Notion OS, with Matt spending only 4–6 hours of judgment per engagement. Target: month 18, not month 6.

## 6. Abundance, defined operationally

Sullivan/Hardy define abundance as *expanding capacity in four lenses*, not "more." What each looks like at GVC in 18 months:

**Time.** Both founders ≤30 hrs/week with at least one no-screens day each weekend. Two real vacations/year (one ≥10 days) where the business runs without them. No client emergency in the past 90 days that broke a weekend.

**Money.** Founders paid $200–250k each in W-2 + distributions — the baseline that makes the rest real. Six months of operating expenses in cash. Retainer MRR ≥$15k covering fixed costs so no engagement is taken under duress. Zero personal-credit-card debt subsidizing the business.

**Relationships.** Three Whos who'd say in a reference call "this is the best contract I've had in years." Five-to-eight past clients texting referrals unprompted. A peer network of 3–5 founders running similar businesses, genuine not networking. Both founders see family weekly without rescheduling.

**Purpose.** Every engagement we sign is one where both founders are unambiguously excited at kickoff. The "what we won't do" page has actually disqualified ≥10 prospects in the past year — disqualifications real, not theater. At least one piece of public work per year made for the love of making it, no commercial KPI attached.

Operational test: hit 18 months with any one missing and the business is broken regardless of the P&L.

## 7. Honest tensions

Where the doctrine will fight reality at GVC's scale.

**1. "Who Not How" assumes capital. We bootstrap.** Who #1 is affordable from month-2 cash flow but Who #3 will require revenue growth or an uncomfortable founder pay cut in months 6–9. Resolution: hire against *committed pipeline*, not aspirational. Slower than Sullivan prescribes, more durable.

**2. 10x rhetoric vs. SMB pricing reality.** The 10x frame originated for entrepreneurs serving high-margin clients. We will hit price resistance at $60k that an enterprise-serving Sullivan student would not. Resolution: 10x lives in *engagement design and fractional team*, not in 10x-ing client price. The quietest place the doctrine over-promises.

**3. Unique Ability vs. founder-as-only-engineer.** Matt's Unique Ability includes "final judgment on every shipped build" — sounds like something a Who can absorb but can't, because that judgment is what clients pay for. Non-removable founder bottleneck on quality. Resolution: cap concurrent engagements at 6 (standard) or 4 (enterprise)/quarter; refuse to grow past it without a peer-level engineering Who. The cap is a feature for our positioning.

**4. Gap-and-Gain vs. competitive pressure.** The Gain frame can mask under-performance by flattering a starting point. If a well-funded competitor eats our segment, "we 3x'd from where we started" doesn't matter. Resolution: keep one Gap-style metric in the quarterly review — "what would a $5M-funded competitor be doing that we aren't?" — held separate from the Gain dashboard. One slide, one minute.

**5. Self-Multiplying Company vs. the "two humans" promise.** Our positioning sells named-human accountability. Self-multiplying Whos dilute that. Resolution: Melissa on every kickoff, Matt on every final review; Whos named publicly on the team page. Narrative evolves from "two humans" to "two named partners and a team they hand-picked." A brand cost we accept.

**6. ABC discipline vs. founder identity.** Both founders have B-column activities they're attached to (Matt: writing every line of build code; Melissa: personally onboarding every client). Sullivan says drop them. Resolution: plan for 9–12 months of slow drop, not 90 days; don't treat the slow drop as failure.

## How this informs the rest of the business plan

- **Revenue model** must be three productized SKUs (Audit / 90-Day Build / Ops Partner). Anything custom-priced violates §1 and §5.
- **Capacity model** caps concurrent engagements at 4–6. Modeling 12 concurrent is the 2x grind in disguise.
- **GTM** is editorial + Melissa's hospitality network + the disqualification page. Outbound sales forbidden in year 1.
- **Hiring** sequences Who #1 → #2 → #3 on a *committed-pipeline* trigger, not a calendar trigger.
- **Financial projection** must show founder pay scaling *before* gross revenue. Founders earning $120k each at $1.5M revenue is a tell the wrong people are getting paid.
- **Pricing must be published.** No published numbers means we are still in the old "free discovery call" mode and §1 hasn't actually happened.
- **"What we won't do" page** must list real disqualifications; without it the philosophy has no enforcement layer.
- **Editorial cadence** ≥1 published artifact every 2 weeks once Who #3 is in place. Slower means the brand isn't compounding.
- **The December annual review is non-negotiable.** Skip it once and the philosophy quietly becomes wall art.
