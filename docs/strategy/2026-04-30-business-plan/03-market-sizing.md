## TL;DR

The "$10 trillion implementation layer" claim is rhetorical, not analytical — it conflates McKinsey's *global productivity-value* estimate ($6.1–$7.9T/yr from generative AI, including all knowledge-worker uplift) with the much smaller *services revenue* available to implementation consultants. A defensible US-only number for the addressable AI-implementation **services** spend among small businesses is **$60–110B/year by 2028** — still enormous, and an order of magnitude above what is being booked today.

For Good Vibes Coding (GVC), a 2-person advisory shop:

- **TAM (US SMBs that could buy AI advisory):** ~3.6M employer firms, $200K–$10M revenue → **$72B/yr** addressable services spend at maturity
- **SAM (filtered to GVC's fit):** ~520K firms (employer, $200K–$5M, English, hospitality/F&B/professional services/regional service biz/e-commerce verticals) → **$5.2B/yr** at $10K avg ARPU
- **SOM (realistic capture):** Year 1 = $180K (8 active clients), Year 3 = $720K (28 clients on retainer + project), Year 5 = $1.8M (firm of 6, ~60 active clients) — capacity-bound, not demand-bound
- **Abundance ceiling at SOM Year 5:** $1.8–2.4M revenue, $700K–$1.0M founder cash + distributions combined, 35–45% EBITDA margin, team of 5–6

A defensible $5.2B SAM and $1.8M Year-5 SOM is more than enough to fund the Sullivan/Hardy-style abundance the founders want.

## The "$10T" claim — traced and corrected

The "$10 trillion implementation layer" phrasing does not appear in any published Cuban interview or McKinsey/IDC/Gartner report we could locate. It originates in the Cuban April 2026 thread comments, where a responder rounded up two distinct figures:

1. **McKinsey (Jun 2023, reaffirmed 2025):** Generative AI could create **$2.6–4.4T/yr** in direct value across 63 use cases, and **$6.1–7.9T/yr** when including broader knowledge-worker productivity. This is *value created*, not *services spend*. (McKinsey, *The economic potential of generative AI*)
2. **Gartner (Sep 2025, updated Jan 2026):** Worldwide AI spending will reach **$1.5T in 2025** and **$2.5T in 2026**, of which roughly $1.87T is IT services and $1.44T is software. This is *all AI spending globally*, dominated by hyperscaler infra and enterprise software — only a fraction is SMB-addressable advisory work.

Neither number supports a "$10T US SMB implementation services" framing. The honest US-only translation:

- Gartner: US AI spending will reach **~$300B in 2026** (~12% of global AI spend × US share of IT spend). SMB share of IT services is historically ~20–25%, suggesting **$60–75B/yr** in US SMB AI-related spend by 2026, only a slice of which is *advisory* (vs. tools, infra, internal labor).
- McKinsey: If 5% of the $4.4T global value is captured by US SMBs ($220B in productivity), and 10–20% of that flows through external advisors as services revenue (a generous services-attach ratio), the **US SMB AI-advisory TAM is $20–45B/yr today, growing toward $60–110B/yr by 2028**.

That range — **tens of billions, not trillions** — is the defensible frame. It is still a category-defining opportunity for a 2-person shop that needs to capture <0.01% to be life-changing.

## TAM build

**Definition:** All US small businesses that *could* eventually buy AI implementation/advisory services, sized as annual addressable spend.

| Segment | Count | Annual addressable spend / firm | Segment TAM | Source / logic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nonemployer firms (sole props, 1099s) | 30.4M | $0–500 (mostly tool subs, not advisory) | ~$3B | SBA Office of Advocacy, Jun 2025; JPMC Institute median spend $28/mo |
| Employer firms, <$200K revenue (very small) | ~1.2M | $500–1,500 (light advisory, mostly DIY + group programs) | ~$1.2B | SUSB 2022 receipts-size tables; SBA "Declining Receipts" issue brief 2024 |
| **Employer firms, $200K–$1M revenue** | **~2.1M** | **$3K–8K** (occasional project + light retainer) | **~$11B** | SUSB 2022; mirrors SCORE/SBDC + indie consultant pricing |
| **Employer firms, $1M–$5M revenue** | **~1.3M** | **$12K–30K** (true ICP — quarterly project + monthly retainer)| **~$28B** | SUSB 2022; aligns w/ marketing-agency retainer band ($1.5K–5K/mo) |
| **Employer firms, $5M–$10M revenue** | **~250K** | **$30K–80K** (fractional CTO band) | **~$14B** | SUSB 2022; fractional CTO retainers $3–10K/mo (TLVTech, CTOx 2025) |
| Employer firms, >$10M revenue | ~150K | $80K+ (mid-market boutique territory — not GVC TAM) | excluded | Out of GVC scope; Xcelacore/AI REV territory |

**TAM total (employer firms, $200K–$10M revenue): ~3.6M firms × $20K blended addressable spend ≈ $72B/yr at maturity.**

**Comparable-spend sanity checks:**
- US **management consulting industry** revenue from SMBs: ~$60B/yr (Statista 2024 — full consulting industry $329B, SMB share ~18%).
- US **digital marketing services** spend by SMBs: ~$50B/yr (typical retainer $1.5K–5K/mo × ~1M firms buying).
- US **fractional executive market** (CFO/CTO/CMO): ~$8–12B/yr and growing 30%+ YoY (k38 Consulting 2025; CTOx 2025).

A **$72B addressable services spend** sits comfortably between digital marketing ($50B) and full-consulting-industry SMB share ($60B), and is consistent with Gartner's $300B US AI spend forecast (services portion). Defensible.

## SAM build (filters applied)

GVC cannot serve all 3.6M TAM firms. SAM filters to firms where the founders' positioning, vertical fit, geography, and price point genuinely fit:

| Filter | Cuts to | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Start: TAM employer firms, $200K–$10M | 3.6M | Baseline |
| **Revenue band $200K–$5M** (drop $5M+ — that's mid-market boutique territory, drop sub-$200K — no advisory budget) | 3.4M | GVC pricing fits a $1M–$5M business; sub-$200K is SCORE/SBDC's lane |
| **English-speaking, US-based** (cuts ~10% Spanish-dominant micro-businesses better served by Latino-focused advisors) | 3.1M | Cultural fit; GVC has no localized Spanish offering |
| **2–50 employees** (drops single-employee employer firms that mostly behave like nonemployers) | 2.0M | JPMC + NFIB show this is the "operational-complexity" cohort |
| **Verticals where Matt's engineering OR Melissa's hospitality-ops experience compounds:** hospitality / F&B, professional services (legal, accounting, agency, consulting), regional service businesses (home services, wellness, fitness), e-commerce $1–5M GMV, light specialty retail | ~520K | See breakdown below — these are the verticals where the founders' resumes create earned authority |
| **Already using SaaS / has digital footprint** (filters out fully-analog operations where AI advisory ROI is negative) | ~470K (proxied — 90% of the above per BetterCloud SaaS-density data) | Owner needs an existing tech stack to integrate AI into |

**SAM = ~470–520K firms × $10K avg ARPU = $4.7–5.2B/yr.**

(ARPU = $10K reflects the blended midpoint of GVC's likely product mix: $5–8K diagnostic projects, $1.5–3K/mo retainers, $10–25K implementation packages — see ARPU section.)

## SOM scenarios (Year 1 / 3 / 5)

A 2-person advisory shop is **capacity-bound**, not demand-bound. The constraint is hours: each active client retainer consumes 8–15 hrs/mo of a partner's time; project work consumes 30–80 hrs over 4–8 weeks. With both founders at 40 productive billable hrs/wk, the firm has ~3,500–4,000 billable hours/yr (after subtracting marketing, admin, learning).

Capacity math:
- 1 retainer client = ~120 hrs/yr → 2 founders can run ~28–32 retainer clients max
- 1 implementation project = ~50 hrs → can run ~70 projects/yr if pure project
- Realistic blend (Year 3): 20 retainers (2,400 hrs) + 12 projects (600 hrs) = 3,000 hrs, sustainable

| Year | Active clients (retainer + project) | Avg ARPU | Revenue | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Year 1 (2026 launch)** | 8 (3 retainers + 5 projects) | $22K | **$180K** | Network-led; mostly via Matt's engineering network + Melissa's hospitality contacts. Underutilized capacity; spend ~30% of time on content/marketing |
| **Year 2** | 16 (8 retainers + 8 projects) | $25K | **$400K** | First brand-driven inbound; one vertical case study published per quarter |
| **Year 3** | 28 (18 retainers + 10 projects) | $26K | **$720K** | Capacity-near-max for 2 founders. Hire 1 implementation associate at end of year |
| **Year 4** | ~45 (firm of 4) | $28K | **$1.25M** | 2 founders + 2 associates. Founders mostly on diagnosis + senior advisory |
| **Year 5** | ~60 (firm of 5–6) | $30K | **$1.8M** | Mature 2-pod structure: hospitality pod (Melissa) + engineering/data pod (Matt). EBITDA margin ramping toward 40% |

**SOM as % of SAM:** Year 5 SOM ($1.8M) / SAM ($5.2B) = **0.035%**. This is a *tiny* slice of the addressable market — which is the point. Capacity, not demand, is the binding constraint.

**Cross-check:** Indie SMB AI consultancies on the AI Journal 2026 list run $0.5–2.5M revenue at 3–8 people. GVC's Year-5 plan sits in the middle of that band — empirically achievable.

## ARPU / LTV ranges

GVC's product mix (per the founder positioning):

| Engagement type | Price | Length | Annual revenue/client |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI Diagnostic Sprint (one-time) | $4,500–8,000 | 3–4 wks | One-shot |
| Implementation Project (single workflow built + deployed) | $10,000–25,000 | 6–10 wks | One-shot |
| Monthly Advisory Retainer | $1,500–3,500/mo | Ongoing | $18K–42K/yr |
| Quarterly Strategy Engagement | $6,000–12,000/qtr | Ongoing | $24K–48K/yr |
| Done-with-you build sprint (productized) | $7,500 fixed | 2 wks | One-shot |

**Blended ARPU realistic ranges:**
- Year 1 (project-heavy mix): $18–25K
- Year 3 (retainer-heavy mix): $25–30K
- Year 5 (mature mix incl. larger retainers + multi-pod accounts): $28–35K

**Comparable benchmarks:**
- Marketing agency retainer for SMBs: $1,500–5,000/mo (Databox, F22 Labs 2025) — GVC's $1,500–3,500/mo retainer band sits in the meaty middle
- Fractional CTO retainer: $3,000–10,000/mo (TLVTech, CTOx 2025) — GVC priced ~30% below to reflect SMB band
- Independent consultant ARPC (avg revenue per client): $30K–60K/yr (Consulting Success 2025 study; n=2,000 consultants) — GVC's $25–30K is appropriate for an SMB-focused shop, lower than enterprise indies

**LTV ranges (gross revenue, 60% gross margin assumed):**

| Retention scenario | Avg life | LTV (revenue) | LTV (gross profit) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pessimistic (project-only, 12-mo avg) | 1.0 yr | $22K | $13K |
| Realistic (retainer mix, 2.5-yr avg) | 2.5 yr | $65K | $39K |
| Optimistic (deep operator relationships, 4-yr avg) | 4.0 yr | $110K | $66K |

**Realistic LTV/CAC target:** With brand-led acquisition (low paid spend, high content investment), CAC should run $1.5K–3K per client. LTV/CAC ratio of 13–22× at the realistic case — strong, and driven by retention more than ARPU expansion.

## Highest-value sub-segments

Within SAM, four sub-segments stand out on count × WTP × reach × fit:

| Sub-segment | US count (proxy) | WTP (annual) | Reach | Fit (1–5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hospitality / F&B operators** (multi-unit restaurants, boutique hotels, bars/breweries, $1–5M revenue) | ~85K | $15–35K | Med-High (Melissa's network + trade media) | **5** — Melissa's 20yr hospitality ops resume is a primary moat; vertical SaaS (Toast, Resy) leaves strategic advisory gap |
| **Professional services 5–25 employees** (boutique law, accounting, agencies, RIA/wealth advisors, $750K–5M revenue) | ~210K | $12–30K | High (LinkedIn, peer referrals, BNI/Vistage) | **4** — Matt's engineering credibility + the cohort is digitally native and budget-equipped; competition from regional agencies |
| **Regional service businesses** (HVAC/plumbing/electrical multi-truck, home services, wellness/fitness studios, $1–5M revenue) | ~140K | $10–25K | Medium (industry trade groups, ServiceTitan ecosystem) | **4** — Operations-heavy; AI integration ROI is concrete (dispatch, inquiry handling, retention); fits Matt's systems thinking |
| **E-commerce $1–5M GMV** (Shopify operators, niche DTC) | ~95K | $12–28K | High (Twitter/X, Shopify community) | **3** — Crowded with AI tool vendors; harder to differentiate; Matt's depth helps with custom integrations but Melissa's ops moat is irrelevant |

**Total high-fit sub-segment count: ~530K firms** — confirms the SAM math from the other direction.

**Strategic implication:** GVC should lead with **hospitality/F&B + regional service businesses** (the Melissa moat) for Year 1–2, then expand to professional services as the brand matures. E-commerce is a bonus channel via inbound, not a primary segment to invest in.

## "Abundance" math at SOM ceiling

At Year 5 ($1.8M revenue, 5–6 person firm):

| Line item | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $1.8M | 60 active clients, $30K blended ARPU |
| Direct delivery cost (associate salaries, contractors, software) | $720K | 40% — typical for boutique at this size |
| Gross profit | $1.08M | 60% margin (in line with high-performing boutiques per Mosaic 2024 benchmarks) |
| Marketing / content | $90K | 5% — content-led, low paid spend |
| G&A (rent, legal, tools, ops) | $90K | 5% |
| Founder salaries (W2 base, before profit) | $300K | $150K each — modest base allows margin retention |
| **EBITDA** | **$600K** | **33% EBITDA margin** — top quartile per Mosaic 2024 |
| Founder distributions (split) | $480K | After-tax founder take-home: ~$300–340K combined |
| Total founder cash (W2 + distributions, combined) | **$780K** | Matches the "abundance" target |

**Stress test — upper end (lucky breaks, premium positioning):**
- If GVC lands 3–5 multi-unit hospitality groups at $60–90K/yr (premium accounts), Year 5 revenue ramps to **$2.4M**, EBITDA margin to 40%+, founder combined take-home ~$1.0M. This is the genuine abundance ceiling for a **disciplined 2-pod, 6-person shop** without raising capital or aggressively scaling team.

**What "abundance" looks like at this ceiling:**
- Founders working ~30–35 hrs/wk on senior advisory + content (the "creative work" Sullivan/Hardy describe), with associates carrying delivery
- Travel and hospitality flexibility built into the calendar
- A clear public brand and category position ("AI advisory for main street")
- Optionality: license the methodology, write a book, sell the practice, or hold and compound

**What it does NOT require:** venture capital, hiring beyond ~6 (delivery quality breaks at 8+ for a 2-founder advisory), geographic expansion, or a SaaS pivot.

The $1.8–2.4M / $600–1,000K EBITDA / 6-person ceiling is a **deliberately bounded** business — the right answer for two founders who want abundance, not empire.
