This is how we run the shop. Read it end-to-end once, then keep it open in a tab. We rewrite it when something changes. We don't add jargon to make it feel professional.

The two non-negotiables before any of the rest matters:
1. **Unique Ability work first.** Matt's judgment + Mel's operator credibility get the morning. Admin is batched. (`02-sullivan-hardy`.)
2. **Both geographies, both founders.** Workshops require both faces. The 2-week PB / 2-week Pocs rotation is the spine. (`22-cold-acquisition-gtm`.)

## Part 2 — The week in the life

A standard 7-day week. Each day has 3 outcomes that have to happen and 1 anti-pattern.

### Monday — outreach + podcast lead day

**Outcomes:**
1. Week's outreach plan written (10 cold contacts named per geo)
2. Podcast Mon ep recorded + shipped
3. Calendly bookings for the week confirmed (intake forms sent)

**Anti-pattern:** opening Slack/email before the morning build block. Inbox runs us instead of we run it.

### Tuesday — delivery + outreach

**Outcomes:**
1. Two sprint build blocks (one client minimum)
2. 8 cold-outreach Looms / emails sent
3. 1–2 discovery calls completed and logged in CRM

**Anti-pattern:** taking a "quick call" that wasn't on the calendar.

### Wednesday — workshop or sample-build day

**Outcomes:**
1. Workshop runs (during workshop week) OR a sample-build Loom shipped (during build week)
2. 3 follow-ups from the prior week closed (proposal, contract, or a "no thanks")
3. Podcast Wed ep recorded + shipped

**Anti-pattern:** soloing the workshop. The pairing IS the brand. (`22-gtm` anti-patterns.)

### Thursday — delivery + content publication

**Outcomes:**
1. Two sprint build blocks
2. One essay or long Loom shipped publicly (LinkedIn + site)
3. Newsletter draft locked for Friday send

**Anti-pattern:** writing without shipping. A draft that doesn't ship by Thursday EOD doesn't count.

### Friday — review + planning + Mel's listener segment + outreach close-out

**Outcomes:**
1. Friday "What Made You Smile" listener segment recorded + shipped (Mel-led)
2. Newsletter sent (recap + 1 CTA, never a deck)
3. Weekly review done — Gain question answered before any planning

**Anti-pattern:** starting the weekly review with "what's behind?" — banned sentence.

### Saturday — deep build (founder choice)

**Outcomes:**
1. One thing — the hardest engineering problem of the week (Matt) OR newsletter + market visit (Mel)
2. One essay drafted (Matt) OR one workshop dry-run (Mel, if the next workshop is the following week)
3. Off by mid-afternoon. Family time begins.

**Anti-pattern:** treating Saturday as overflow Friday. If Friday's work didn't ship, it doesn't get rescued on Saturday.

### Sunday — family / off / quarterly review only

**Outcomes:**
1. Genuinely off (no client work, no Slack)
2. Light reading allowed (industry, not client)
3. Quarterly review only on the last Sunday of the quarter

**Anti-pattern:** "just one email." The one email becomes three hours.

## Part 3 — The month in the life (M1 → M9)

### M1 — Ship the offer

- **Theme:** ship the offer
- **Top 3 outcomes:** homepage with 3 starter products live; 2 GBPs claimed and optimized; first sprint sold (target: AI Visibility Tune-Up at $750)
- **Hire:** none yet
- **KPI:** 40+ named cold-outreach conversations
- **Decision-point:** "did we have at least one paid sprint?" If no, audit the offer before M2.
- **Anti-patterns:** redesigning the homepage a third time; hiring before there's revenue; "AI consulting" in any pitch language
- **Geography:** 70% PB / 30% Pocs (PB residency, Pocs by Zoom)

### M2 — First sprints sold

- **Theme:** first sprints sold
- **Top 3 outcomes:** 4–6 sprints sold; first retainer offered post-sprint; first Pocs residency week run (workshop in person)
- **Hire:** fractional bookkeeper ($300–600/mo)
- **KPI:** sprint→retainer conversion rate (target: ≥40% first month)
- **Decision-point:** "did the workshop produce 2+ booked consults?" If no, change the venue or the topic before M3.
- **Anti-patterns:** taking a referral-only mindset; skipping the case-study consent ask at sprint close; over-engineering a sprint deliverable
- **Geography:** 60% PB / 40% Pocs

### M3 — Workshop cadence stable

- **Theme:** workshop cadence stable
- **Top 3 outcomes:** 4 workshops total (2/geo); first published case study; Restaurant Full Stack bundle live on the homepage
- **Hire:** Content + Ops VA ($2,500–3,000/mo)
- **KPI:** workshops/month, sprints/workshop (target: ≥3)
- **Decision-point (the big one — see `23-synthesis` §10):** "is the funnel real? 4+ workshops run, 3+ sprints/workshop, 3+ retainers MRR?" If yes, commit cadence. If no, audit offer + outreach.
- **Anti-patterns:** running solo workshops; writing the case study without client consent; lowering price to close
- **Geography:** 60% PB / 40% Pocs

### M4 — First retainer rhythm

- **Theme:** retainer rhythm in place
- **Top 3 outcomes:** 4 retainers running cleanly; eval harness on every shipped automation; second case study live
- **Hire:** none new (VA absorbing load)
- **KPI:** MRR (target: $4–6K), client churn (target: 0)
- **Decision-point:** "are we proactively serving retainers, or are they just passive? Loom every retainer client this month."
- **Anti-patterns:** treating retainers as set-and-forget; saying yes to scope creep without re-quoting; missing a workshop
- **Geography:** 50% PB / 50% Pocs (rotation now stable)

### M5 — Layer paid + bundle

- **Theme:** layer the bundle, test paid ads
- **Top 3 outcomes:** Restaurant Full Stack sold once; first paid Google Ads test live (PB only); third case study live
- **Hire:** none
- **KPI:** MRR (target: $8K), paid CPL (target: <$120 cold)
- **Decision-point:** "is paid ads converting AI Front-Desk above 3%?" If yes, scale. If no, kill it and rebudget to direct mail / workshops.
- **Anti-patterns:** chasing CPL into low-quality leads; running ads to a generic landing page; selling the bundle without 5 standalone deals first
- **Geography:** 60% PB / 40% Pocs

### M6 — Decision month

- **Theme:** mid-year decision
- **Top 3 outcomes:** $10K+ MRR running; 5+ active retainers; podcast review (attributable leads ≥1/mo or downscale)
- **Hire:** **conditional** — fractional podcast editor ($2,500/mo) IF podcast pulled its weight
- **KPI:** MRR ($10K), workshop run-rate ($30–40K/mo from workshops), founder hours (≤60/wk each)
- **Decision-point:** "$10K+ MRR? Podcast leads ≥1/mo? Founders <60 hr/wk?" Three yeses → continue. One no → tighten.
- **Anti-patterns:** hiring the podcast editor on hope; pushing into a third geography; expanding the offer menu (it stays at 3+2+1)
- **Geography:** 50% PB / 50% Pocs

### M7 — Throughput month

- **Theme:** throughput
- **Top 3 outcomes:** 6+ active retainers; 8 sprints/month sold; 2 workshops/geo running on autopilot via VA
- **Hire:** none new
- **KPI:** MRR ($13K), sprints sold/month (target: 8+)
- **Decision-point:** "is delivery becoming the bottleneck?" If yes, line up junior implementer for M9.
- **Anti-patterns:** taking a sprint that doesn't fit the 3 starter shapes; saying yes to any "custom" work without a 30% custom premium
- **Geography:** 50% PB / 50% Pocs

### M8 — Compound month

- **Theme:** compound the playbook
- **Top 3 outcomes:** 4+ case studies live; 60% of new sprints come pre-warmed by content/SEO/podcast; first GBP hyperlocal #1–3 ranking
- **Hire:** none
- **KPI:** MRR ($16K), inbound % of new sprints (target: ≥40%)
- **Decision-point:** "is the playbook compounding? are we doing things twice as efficiently as M3?" If yes, the IP layer is real.
- **Anti-patterns:** rebuilding pipeline tools; switching CRMs; rewriting the homepage
- **Geography:** 50% PB / 50% Pocs

### M9 — Closing on $20K MRR

- **Theme:** $20K MRR
- **Top 3 outcomes:** $20K MRR hit (or within 10%); junior implementer or Pocs field rep hired (if MRR holds); annual planning weekend booked
- **Hire:** **conditional** — junior implementer (M9–12) if 6+ active sprints/month, OR Pocs field rep ($1,500/mo) if Pocs pipeline matters more than throughput
- **KPI:** MRR ($20K), retention (target: 90%+ over the past 6 months)
- **Decision-point:** "Path A still right? Or do we need to add a peer-level engineer?" Honest answer drives Y2 plan.
- **Anti-patterns:** hiring two people in the same month; opening an office; chasing a third geography
- **Geography:** 50% PB / 50% Pocs

## Part 4 — Quarter rhythm

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

**When:** last Friday of every third month, half-day, off-site (coffee shop, library reading room, anywhere not the home desk).

**Who attends:** founders only through M9. From M10+, the VA joins for the first 60 minutes (operational metrics review) and leaves before the strategic block.

**Agenda (3 hours):**
1. **Gain block (30 min).** Five wins from the quarter. Write them. No problem-solving allowed yet.
2. **Numbers (45 min).** MRR, retention, sprints sold, workshop conversion, podcast leads, founder hours, A-column %. Numbers come from the dashboard (§7), not from feel.
3. **Gap question (15 min).** "What would a $5M-funded competitor be doing that we aren't?" One slide, one minute on each side. Held separate from the Gain dashboard.
4. **The 10x bet (45 min).** One bet for next quarter. Whiteboard one page. Not a deck.
5. **Drop one (15 min).** One C-column item that leaves the calendar this quarter.
6. **Done.** No additional 4-hour deep-dive. The quarter starts Monday.

### Gap-and-Gain monthly review ritual

**When:** last Friday of every month, 90 min, can be at home.

**Format (from `02-sullivan-hardy`):**
- Backwards dashboard: revenue this month vs. last; vs. 12 months ago
- Founder hours actually worked (target ≤30/wk by M9)
- % founder hours in A-column (target ≥70% by M9)
- Three "wins we'd have killed for a year ago" before any problem-solving
- Then forward planning

**Single emotional rule:** no founder review starts with "we should be further ahead." Banned.

### Annual planning weekend

**When:** mid-December, two days. Friday afternoon → Saturday late afternoon.

**Where:** off-site, ideally Pocs (Cocoon Hawley, the Heron in Narrowsburg, or a rented house). Not at home. Not in PB during Art Basel chaos.

**Format:** self-multiplying audit. Every system, asset, Who reviewed for whether it's compounding or merely surviving. Every C-column activity reviewed. The next year's three productized SKUs locked. Pricing reviewed (don't change without data).

## Part 5 — Lifecycle workflows

### 1. Lead capture

**CRM choice: Folk** ($25/seat/mo, 2 seats = $50/mo). Why: it merges Gmail + LinkedIn + Calendly automatically, has a clean group/list model, doesn't try to be a project manager. Notion is too slow for daily ops. Airtable is too DIY for what we need today.

**Steps:**
1. **Cold contact arrives** — via Calendly book, email reply, workshop iPad opt-in, mail-response phone call, or LinkedIn DM. VA (or Matt/Mel before VA) creates Folk record same day.
2. **Required fields:** name, email, phone, business name, geo (PB or Pocs), source (workshop / cold email / mail / referral / podcast / GBP / paid), vertical, employee count (rough), SaaS-stack-spend (rough), first-touch date, status.
3. **Status taxonomy (5 states, no more):** `new` → `qualified` → `proposed` → `won` → `closed-no`. A `dormant` tag for reactivation later.
4. **Calendly link:** `calendly.com/goodvibescoding/discovery-30` — single 30-min slot type. No 60-min discovery. No "let's chat."
5. **Auto-confirmation:** Calendly sends a 3-line confirmation + intake form (Tally, free). Form asks: business name, what SaaS you currently pay for, one sentence on what's not working.
6. **Time:** 5 min per lead to enter. VA absorbs from M3.

### 2. Discovery call

**Format:** 30 min Calendly, video on, no deck.

**Agenda (Mel runs PB hospitality + Pocs lodging; Matt runs everything else):**
1. **0–3 min:** real intro, named humans, ask how their week is.
2. **3–8 min:** "Tell us what's not working" — let them talk. Don't pitch.
3. **8–18 min:** the 5 questions (below).
4. **18–25 min:** if fit — explain the relevant starter sprint in plain words. Quote the price out loud.
5. **25–30 min:** next step. Always one of: (a) we send a one-page scope by Friday, (b) it's not a fit, here's who is, (c) you should come to the workshop next month before deciding.

**The 5 questions:**
1. What software are you currently paying for, and roughly how much?
2. Walk me through what happens when [specific thing in their business] — phone rings / new booking / a review comes in / a menu changes.
3. What's the one task you'd pay to never think about again?
4. If we built this and you owned the code, would your team actually use it?
5. Are you the decision-maker, or is there someone else who needs to be in the next call?

**Decision criteria (good fit):**
- Owner-operated, $250K–$5M revenue
- Pays $200+/mo in SaaS today
- One named adoption owner client-side
- Plain-language answers to questions 1–4
- Internet works at the business

**Decision criteria (bad fit):**
- "We need a chatbot" with no operator pain behind it
- Below $250K revenue (can't sustain retainer)
- Vendor-questionnaire culture (procurement over founder)
- Wants custom pricing without a sprint shape that matches
- Internet is the actual problem

**Follow-up email template (sent within 4 hours):**

```
Subject: Quick recap — [their business]

[Name],

Good talking. Here's what we heard:
- [pain 1]
- [pain 2]
- [the SaaS you'd most like to stop paying for: X]

Next step: [scope by Friday / workshop date / not-a-fit referral].

If anything changed since we hung up, just reply.

— Matt & Mel
goodvibescoding.com
```

### 3. Pilot/sprint sale

**Steps (target: proposal → invoice in 5 business days):**
1. **Day 0 (call day):** discovery call complete, fit confirmed.
2. **Day 1–2:** Matt drafts a one-page scope (Google Doc). Plain language. Three sections: what we're building, what we're not building, what you do client-side. Price stated up front, not buried.
3. **Day 3:** Mel sends scope + Stripe invoice link via email. No e-sign theater. Invoice IS the contract for sprints under $3,500. Above $3,500: short MSA + SOW (DocuSign).
4. **Payment terms:** 50% on signature for sprints under $1,800. 100% on signature for $750 sprints (smaller than the cost of chasing). 50/50 split (signature/handoff) for bundles ($3,500+). Stripe.
5. **Refund policy:** 7-day cancel-and-refund if work hasn't started. Pro-rated if we've shipped any deliverable. No refund after handoff Loom.
6. **Contract template structure (above $3,500):** parties, scope, deliverables, timeline, price + payment, IP ownership (client owns the code), confidentiality (mutual, 2 years), termination (either party, 7-day written), warranty disclaimer, governing law (Florida).

### 4. Sprint kickoff

**Week 1 of delivery.**

**Kickoff call agenda (45 min, video on):**
1. 0–5: introductions if more than one person on their side
2. 5–15: walk through the scope doc again, out loud. Confirm we're aligned.
3. 15–25: credentials and access — what we need from them (logins, Google Business Profile owner access, Stripe API key if applicable). All of it lives in 1Password shared vault.
4. 25–35: communication agreement — Slack Connect channel for async, Loom for daily updates (Mon/Wed/Fri), one weekly 30-min video sync. No email threads.
5. 35–40: what they need to do this week — usually 2 things, no more.
6. 40–45: confirm handoff date.

**Intake form (Tally) — sent before kickoff:**
- All login URLs and admin emails
- Their hours of operation
- Key staff names + roles
- One sample of the artifact we're replacing (menu PDF, current Yext listing, etc.)
- Their #1 worry about the project

**Communication choices:** Slack Connect for async (default). Loom for visual progress. Email only for invoicing or contractual. Phone only if Slack is broken or it's the Pocs.

### 5. Sprint handoff

**Close-out call (45 min) at scheduled handoff date.**

**Steps:**
1. **Live demo (15 min):** show the thing working in their environment.
2. **Loom library handoff (5 min):** every system gets 3 Looms — "how to use it daily," "how to troubleshoot," "when to call us." Linked from a shared Google Doc.
3. **Deliverable package (10 min):** code repo (GitHub, transferred to their org if they have one, or staying in ours with a license), credentials returned, eval harness explained.
4. **Retainer pitch (10 min):** "Here's what stays sharp on its own. Here's what doesn't. We can keep watching it for $[250–500]/mo. Yes / no this week." No "let me think about it" trap — we name the deadline.
5. **Case study consent (5 min):** "Can we write this up? You approve every word. Use your name or anonymize — your call." Get it in writing same day. (Email reply with a literal "yes" is enough.)

**Refusal language for case study:** "Totally fine. We'll mention the vertical and outcome only, no names."

### 6. Retainer ongoing rhythm

**What the client gets monthly:**
- Eval harness pass (automated weekly, summary monthly)
- One 5-min Loom showing what we touched and why
- One 30-min monthly check-in call (calendar invite, recurring)
- Invoice + receipt (Stripe, auto)

**Standing meeting cadence:** monthly 30-min, recurring on the last Tuesday of the month.

**Reporting format:** 1-page Google Doc, same template, three sections: "what's working," "what we touched," "what to watch." Sent 24 hr before the standing meeting.

**Upsell triggers:**
- Client mentions a SaaS we haven't replaced yet → propose a new sprint
- Client adds a location / line of business → propose a "scale" sprint
- 6+ months of clean retention → offer the bundle upgrade if applicable
- Never upsell during a problem call. Solve the problem first; upsell next cycle.

### 7. Workshop production cycle (4-week rolling)

**Week -4 (commit):**
- Venue confirmed (library / Chamber / coworking). Date locked.
- Workshop event on GBP for both geos
- VA submits to library calendar, Chamber newsletter, local FB groups, Nextdoor
- Eventbrite or Tally signup live (cap at 25)

**Week -3 (promote):**
- Two LinkedIn posts (Mel)
- One newsletter mention
- One podcast outro mention
- Direct outreach to 20 named people in the geo who'd benefit

**Week -2 (final push):**
- Reminder posts in local FB groups
- Confirmation email to registered attendees
- Print 30 1-pagers (the "three SaaS we'd cut today" cheatsheet)

**Week -1 (prep):**
- Both founders dry-run the 75-min flow
- iPad opt-in form ready
- Coffee/bagels ordered
- Charge gear, test demo on venue WiFi

**Week 0 (run):**
- 75-min workshop (format in `22-gtm`)
- iPad opt-in at end. 3 individual consults booked on the spot.
- Same-day: VA enters every name into Folk, sends confirmation emails

**Week +1 (convert):**
- 3 individual consults run
- Target: 1–2 paid sprints from the cohort

### 8. Direct mail wave (Pocs) — 2-step cadence

**Cadence:** one wave per quarter (per `22-gtm`), 600 pieces.

**Steps:**
1. **Day -14:** list build (county DBA registries + Chamber roster + Yelp/GBP scrape, 25-mi radius). VA cleans to ~600 mailable.
2. **Day -7:** postcard design finalized (no QR, hand-signed-style headline, B&W founder photos). Print at local printer ($0.65 all-in).
3. **Day -3:** Mel hand-signs all 600 postcards with podcast playing (50/hour).
4. **Day 0:** mailed mid-week, hit mailboxes Fri/Sat.
5. **Day 0–21:** track responses in Folk. Phone is the primary response (rural). Calendly second.
6. **Day +21:** letter wave to non-responders. ~520 letters. First-class stamp, hand-applied. Hand-addressed Sundays.
7. **Day +21–60:** track again. Total response target 3.5–6%.
8. **Day +60:** wave debrief into the quarterly review.

### 9. Podcast production cycle

**Daily (Mon–Thu) — DIY through M5:**
- 4 AM: auto-briefing scrapes (`briefing.md` ready)
- 6:45: Matt reviews briefing, picks 3 stories (30 min)
- 13:00: record with Mel (25–35 min)
- 14:00: edit + level (one founder, 45–75 min)
- 16:00: show-notes + upload (20–30 min)
- VA distributes: audiogram, LinkedIn carousel, newsletter assembly

**Friday — listener segment (5–7 min):**
- Mon–Thu: VA triages submissions
- Thu evening: Mel picks 3 stories
- Fri morning: Mel records solo, ~30 min total

**With fractional editor (M6+):**
- Founders: briefing review (30 min), record (30 min), 15-min review of editor draft. Total ~75 min/day.
- Editor owns: cleanup, levels, music beds, show-notes draft, upload.
- Saves ~6 founder-hours/week.

### 10. Case study production (2-week cycle)

1. **Day 0 (handoff):** consent obtained, basic facts captured.
2. **Day 1:** VA drafts a 3-question intake (numeric outcomes — hours saved, $ saved, calls handled, etc.) and emails the client.
3. **Day 5:** intake returned. Mel writes the 600-word case study (same template every time: situation / what we built / outcome / their words).
4. **Day 7:** client approves every word.
5. **Day 9:** Matt records a 3-min Loom walkthrough of the build (with consent).
6. **Day 10:** publishes to `/case-studies/[geo]/[vertical]/[client-slug]`. JSON-LD schema. Newsletter mention. LinkedIn post. GBP post (both geos if relevant).
7. **Day 14:** thank-you note (mailed, not emailed) to the client.

## Part 8 — Anti-patterns (the "don't" list)

Things we've already seen and committed to refusing.

**Behavioral:**
1. Starting the weekly review with "what's behind?" — banned sentence.
2. Saying "AI consulting" in a cold pitch.
3. Soloing a workshop. The pairing is the brand.
4. Working past 17:00 on weekdays (except workshop days).
5. Saturday as overflow Friday.
6. "Just one email" on Sunday.
7. Apologizing in pitches ("sorry to bother you").

**Operational:**
8. Building a third sprint during a delivery week. Two-sprint cap.
9. Custom-priced anything without a 30%+ custom premium.
10. Free discovery calls longer than 30 minutes.
11. Slack/email before the morning build block.
12. Bespoke proposals — every proposal maps to one of the 3+2+1 SKUs.
13. Notion-as-PM. Linear is the PM tool.
14. Adding a tool without removing one.
15. Hiring before there's revenue to cover the hire for 3 months.
16. Two hires in the same month.
17. Re-platforming the website or switching CRMs once chosen.

**Strategic:**
18. Path C / WikiFold-as-SaaS spinoff. Ruled out.
19. Bronze/Silver/Gold tier subscriptions.
20. Outside capital, equity, or rev-share.
21. A third geography before M9.
22. Opening an office in either market.
23. Sponsorship of the podcast before M18.
24. Customer-service chatbots (88% of customers prefer humans).
25. Google Local Service Ads.
26. QR codes on rural direct mail; bulk-rate indicia on letters.
27. Identical content across geos.
28. Podcast cadence below 4×/week without a structural reason.
29. Selling from the workshop podium.
30. Hiring a salesperson. The funnel stays founder-owned.
31. Replicating Matt at peer level before Y3.
32. Skipping the December annual review. Skip once and the philosophy quietly becomes wall art.
